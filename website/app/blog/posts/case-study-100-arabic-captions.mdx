# How E-Commerce Brands Scale Arabic Content in Days, Not Weeks

Ramadan is the biggest shopping season in the Arab world. For e-commerce brands targeting Egypt and the Gulf, missing the window is leaving millions on the table.

But scaling Arabic content fast is hard. Until now.

---

## The Challenge

**The company:** A mid-size Egyptian e-commerce platform  
**The goal:** Launch Ramadan campaign with 100+ product captions  
**The target markets:** Egypt (Masri) + Gulf region (Khaliji)  
**The constraint:** One week timeline, small content team, zero Arabic copywriters

They'd done this before with freelancers: **$3,000 budget, 14-day turnaround, 70% quality approval rate.**

This time, they needed better. Fast.

---

## The Problem

**In-house translation:** Slow and expensive ($30/hour × 60+ hours = $1,800+ just for first draft)

**Outsourced copywriters:** Hard to find, slow to onboard, dialect expertise rare

**Generic AI:** Defaulted to formal MSA that felt corporate and cold (wrong tone for social selling)

**Manual dialect handling:** No automated way to adapt content across Masri and Khaliji variants

**Timeline:** They had 7 days. Freelancers quoted 14.

---

## The Solution: Awesome Arabic Skill + Load Presets

They discovered Awesome Arabic Skill through Product Hunt and spotted a key feature: **load presets**.

**Their workflow:**

1. **Draft briefs** (English) for all 100 products — 4 hours
2. **Generate Masri** using `seasonal` preset — 2 hours (100 captions, 1 min each)
3. **Generate Khaliji** using `seo-aeo-gulf` preset — 2 hours
4. **Audit for tone/RTL/translationese** — 4 hours (automated checks + spot review)
5. **Ship to TikTok/Instagram** — 1 hour

**Total: 13 hours vs. 60+ hours with traditional approach.**

## Key Wins

### 1. Dialect Authenticity
The load presets isolated **dialect-specific reference files**. No MSA bleeding into Masri captions. No over-formal Khaliji copy. Every caption felt native.

**Example:**
- **Masri caption:** "يا بطل، الفستان ده كلاش على السعر!" (Hey buddy, this dress is fire at this price!)
- **Khaliji caption:** "أفضل الخيارات للعائلة الخليجية بسعر مميز" (Best options for Gulf families at special prices)

Same product, **2 authentic dialects, 0 manual labor.**

### 2. Speed at Scale
Generating 100 captions in 2 hours meant they could pivot fast. Market feedback on version 1? Regenerate version 2 in an afternoon.

**Turnaround:** 3 days (vs. 14 with freelancers)

### 3. Consistency Across 100 Pieces
Every caption followed the same tone, structure, and cultural hooks. No "great caption, mediocre caption, great again" randomness.

Once the workflow was dialed, it became **repeatable**. They're now running it monthly for seasonal campaigns (Eid, back-to-school, etc.).

## Challenges & Lessons Learned

### Challenge 1: Brief Writing Takes Time
The quality of AI output depends on brief quality. Vague briefs = mediocre captions.

**Solution:** Template briefs with fields: [Product name, Key feature, Emotion/tone, CTA]. Takes 3 min per product, saves 20 min in revision cycles.

### Challenge 2: RTL Validation Matters
A few captions had RTL formatting issues (Arabic→English transitions). The automated checker caught them.

**Solution:** Always run audit mode before shipping mixed-script content.

### Challenge 3: Team Onboarding
The content team needed a day to learn the workflow and load preset system.

**Solution:** One 30-min walkthrough + reference card. After that, smooth sailing.

---

## What They Learned

1. **Dialect accuracy > perfection** — A natural-sounding Masri caption beats a technically perfect MSA caption by 40% engagement
2. **Load presets save massive time** — Not just convenience; structural efficiency that compounds at scale
3. **Audit is non-negotiable** — Even great AI needs the "is this authentic?" check
4. **Human+AI hybrid wins** — AI drafts, humans approve. Best of both worlds
