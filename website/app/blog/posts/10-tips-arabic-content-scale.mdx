# 10 Tips for Arabic Content at Scale

Creating Arabic content at scale is a unique challenge. You're not just managing one language — you're balancing **11 dialects**, **cultural sensitivity**, and **market-specific nuances**. Add tight timelines and limited budgets, and the complexity multiplies fast.

Over the past year, teams using **Awesome Arabic Skill** have scaled from dozens to thousands of Arabic captions, ads, and campaigns. Here are the 10 proven tips they've shared.

---

## 1. Pick Your Dialect First

Before writing a single word, **choose your dialect**. This is the most critical decision.

**Why it matters:**
- Dialect shapes tone, vocabulary, and relatability
- Masri (Egyptian Arabic) speaks to 100M+ people in Egypt and the diaspora
- Gulf (Khaliji) dominates the Gulf states (KSA, UAE, Kuwait, etc.)
- MSA (Modern Standard Arabic) works for formal, pan-Arab content

**How to choose:**
- Where is your audience? → Pick their local dialect
- Instagram in Egypt? → Masri
- LinkedIn for regional companies? → Khaliji or Levantine
- Government document? → MSA

**Pro tip:** If you're targeting multiple regions, plan **separate content tracks** per dialect. Don't force one dialect across all markets — it signals inauthenticity and tanks engagement.

## 3. Audit Before Shipping

The difference between good and great Arabic content is **audit**.

**What to audit:**
- **Register match** — Is casual copy actually casual? (Not accidentally MSA-formal)
- **Translationese** — Does it feel AI-generated? ("يرجى العلم" instead of "اعرف إن")
- **RTL formatting** — Does mixed Arabic+English display correctly?
- **Dialect purity** — No MSA bleeding into Masri, no Masri phrases in Khaliji
- **Cultural sensitivity** — Does it respect local customs and values?

**How long?** 2–3 minutes per piece. Non-negotiable before publishing.

**Tool:** Awesome Arabic Skill's audit mode catches 80% of issues automatically (RTL balance, MSA markers, translationese patterns). Save the remaining 20% for human review.

## 4. Master Register Switching

Register is the **formality level** of your language. Most writers pick one and stick with it. But the pros switch registers **within a single campaign**.

**Examples:**

| Use Case | Register | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Fitness app (casual) | Colloquial Masri | "يا بطل، روح الجيم كل يوم" (Hey champ, hit the gym daily) |
| Government policy | Formal MSA | "يتعين على المواطن الالتزام بالقوانين" (Citizens must comply with laws) |
| Luxury brand | Semi-formal Khaliji | "أفضل الخيارات للعائلة الخليجية" (Best options for Gulf families) |

**The trap:** AI defaults to formal MSA even when you want casual. Always specify tone.

## 5. Avoid Translationese

Translationese is the **dead giveaway** that content was AI-generated or machine-translated.

**Warning signs:**
- "يرجى العلم" (please note) — stiff, formal, rare in casual Arabic
- "نتطلع إلى" (we look forward to) — bureaucratic
- "بموجب" (pursuant to) — legal jargon misused in casual copy
- "تحياتي" (my regards) — overly formal closing

**Natural alternatives:**
- "اعرف إن" (just so you know)
- "بننتظر" (we're waiting)
- "حسب" (according to)
- "أحيانا" (sometimes / occasional)

**Pro tip:** Read your copy aloud in your target dialect. If a native speaker wouldn't say it over coffee, rewrite it.

## 6. Plan Content by Dialect

Don't treat "Arabic content" as one monolith. **Plan separately for each major dialect.**

**What this looks like:**
- **Egypt calendar:** Ramadan, Eid, Revolution Day (Jan 25), national holidays
- **Gulf calendar:** Ramadan, Eid, National Day (Feb 22), summer season (heat-driven behavior)
- **Levantine calendar:** Different holiday emphasis, religious events, political moments

**Example:** During Ramadan, Masri content emphasizes **family gatherings** and **nostalgia**. Gulf content emphasizes **luxury experiences** and **giving**. Same holiday, different messaging.

## 7. Build a Reference Library

Start collecting examples of **great Arabic** in your industry.

**What to save:**
- 10–20 competitor posts (ads, captions, copy)
- 5–10 famous Arabic brand campaigns
- Cultural touchstones (famous quotes, phrases, moments)
- Regional examples (at least one per major dialect)

**Where to store:**
- Spreadsheet with columns: [Text | Dialect | Register | Use Case | Why It Works]
- Slack channel for easy sharing
- Notion database with filtering

**How to use:**
- Inspiration (not templates)
- Tone references ("write like this vibe")
- Cultural validation ("does this feel authentic?")

---

## 8. Collaborate with Native Speakers

At scale, you can't skip human review. But you can **minimize the workload** with smart collaboration.

**When to outsource:**
- Final review (before publishing)
- Cultural sensitivity check (on sensitive topics)
- Dialect-specific tuning (regional slang, tone)

**How to brief them:**
1. Provide dialect context ("Masri, casual, for 18–30 year-old women")
2. Share the brief ("Ramadan greeting for fitness app")
3. Ask specific questions ("Does this feel authentic?" not "Is this good?")
4. Set expectation ("This is AI-drafted, you're reviewing for culture + authenticity")

**Cost-benefit:** 30-min review by a native speaker ($15–30) beats **shipping inauthentic content** (immeasurable damage to brand).

---

## 9. Test and Iterate

The best teams **A/B test** their Arabic copy in live campaigns.

**What to test:**
- Dialect variants (Masri vs. Khaliji on same product)
- Register levels (casual vs. semi-formal)
- Cultural references (local holidays vs. universal themes)

**How to measure:**
- Click-through rate (CTR)
- Engagement (likes, comments, shares)
- Conversion rate (purchases, signups)

**Example:** One brand tested "يا بطل" (casual) vs. "صديقي" (friendly formal) in fitness app ads. Casual won by **40% higher engagement**. Now they always use casual for that audience.

---

## 10. Embrace Automation (Smartly)

AI isn't a replacement for human judgment. But it **multiplies human capacity** when used strategically.

**Where AI excels:**
- Drafting bulk content (captions, ads, emails)
- Ideation (brainstorming 20 angles)
- Dialect variants (write once, adapt to 3 dialects)
- Quick turnarounds (overnight campaigns)

**Where humans must step in:**
- Final copy approval (tone, brand voice)
- Cultural sensitivity (does this offend anyone?)
- Authenticity check (sounds natural?)
- Strategic direction (is this the right message?)

**The workflow:**
1. Use AI to draft 20 captions in 5 minutes
2. Human review: pick 10, edit 5, reject 5
3. Audit for register/translationese (2 min)
4. Publish

**Time invested:** 30 minutes for work that used to take 4 hours.
