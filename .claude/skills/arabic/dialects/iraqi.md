# 🇮🇶 عراقي — Iraqi Arabic

**Population:** ~42 million | **Diaspora:** Large communities in UK, Sweden, Jordan, UAE, Turkey, USA
**Sub-dialects:** Baghdadi (urban prestige), Basrawi (Southern, softer), Mosuli (Northern, more formal), Anaezi (tribal)
**When to use:** Iraq-targeted content, Iraqi diaspora content, regional campaigns in the Middle East

---

## 1. Sub-Dialect Map

### بغدادي (Baghdadi — Capital, prestige)
The dominant commercial and media dialect. Use as default for Iraqi content.
- Fastest urban evolution
- Most media exposure (Iraqi TV, YouTube channels)
- Code-switching with English in professional contexts

### بصراوي (Basrawi — Southern Iraq)
- Softer consonants, more melodic rhythm
- Gulf influence (close to Kuwait/KSA border)
- Oil industry vocabulary
- Strong Persian loanwords in some areas

### موصلي (Mosuli — Northern Iraq / Mosul)
- More classical Arabic features preserved
- Some Kurdish vocabulary integration
- Post-ISIS: emotional sensitivity required around city references
- Northern Iraq (Kurdistan region): separate Kurdish audience — different dialect entirely

### Tribal / Rural
- Strong tribal vocabulary (qabila, 'ashira)
- Honor/family honor concepts central
- Poetry is deeply valued — quoting poetry earns respect

## 2. Phonology — Sound Rules

| Feature | Iraqi Arabic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ق (qaf) | g in Baghdad | "قلب" → "galb" (not glottal stop like Masri) |
| ج (jeem) | g (urban) / ch (some areas) | "جميل" → "gmiil" (Baghdadi) |
| ك | sometimes ch | "كيف" → "cheef" (Gulf influence in south) |
| گ (gaf) | Unique Iraqi sound | گلبي = قلبي — written with گ to indicate |
| ذ → z / ث → s | Common simplification | like other dialects |
| Long ā | preserved longer than Masri | |

**The گ sound is the single clearest Iraqi marker.** "گلبي" (my heart), "گاعد" (sitting), "گلتلك" (I told you).

## 4. Grammar Patterns

### Present tense (گاعد or أكو marker)
Iraqi uses گاعد (sitting) to indicate ongoing present action:
- "گاعد يكتب" = he is writing right now
- Simple "يكتب" = he writes (habitual)
- This is the Iraqi equivalent of the Egyptian بـ prefix

### Future tense
- رح + verb: "رح أكتب" = I will write
- بس + verb: "بس أكتب" = when I write / as soon as I write

### Negation
- ما + verb: "ما كتب" = didn't write
- مو: "مو صح" = not right
- ماكو: "ماكو وقت" = there is no time
- No double negation sandwich (not Iraqi)

### Unique features
- أكو / ماكو (there is / there isn't) — completely unique to Iraqi Arabic
- These words alone identify the dialect immediately
- "أكو موضوع لازم نحچيه" = there's a topic we need to talk about

## 7. Brand Voice Guide

### Consumer brands (Iraq)
- Warm, generous register — generosity (كرم) is a core Iraqi value
- Direct and honest — Iraqis are skeptical of corporate polish and over-promise
- Humor works: Iraqi dark comedy is famous across the Arab world; self-deprecating humor earns trust
- Proverbs and wisdom: Iraqi culture values poetic expression and classical references
- Family-first framing always resonates

### Food brands
- أكل عراقي references: قوزي, تمن (rice), گوزي, مرق — these trigger genuine pride
- Home cooking / traditional recipe angle outperforms restaurant polish
- "زي أكل الأم" (like mother's cooking) = highest praise

### Diaspora content (Iraqi Arabs in UK, Sweden, Jordan)
- Nostalgia hook: highly powerful — homeland longing is acute in Iraqi diaspora
- Music, food, and specific place names (Mutanabbi Street, Palms of Basra) = identity triggers
- Political sensitivity: avoid 2003 invasion, Saddam era, ISIS references in commercial content

### B2B / professional services
- L4 Baghdadi professional register
- Iraq is rebuilding: construction, energy, telecom, fintech = active sectors
- "نبني مستقبل العراق" type framing resonates with ambitious professionals
- Be specific about experience with Iraqi regulatory environment

## 8. Cultural Context

### Dark humor tradition
Iraqis are famous for dark, self-aware humor about hardship. It's a survival mechanism and a bonding tool.
- Dark humor does NOT mean disrespect — it's a mark of genuine connection
- Example tone: acknowledging difficulties with a wink, not pretending everything is fine
- Brands that use this earn deep loyalty. Brands that ignore it sound out of touch.

### Poetry and literature
Iraq is the birthplace of Arabic poetry. Al-Mutanabbi is quoted like proverbs.
- A well-placed line of poetry in brand copy = immediate credibility with educated Iraqis
- Even half-lines are recognized: "على قدر أهل العزم..." is universally known

### Religious and ethnic diversity
- Iraq has Shia/Sunni/Christian/Yazidi/Kurdish communities
- For national brands: maintain strict religion neutrality in commercial content
- Avoid Shia-specific or Sunni-specific markers unless targeting a specific community
