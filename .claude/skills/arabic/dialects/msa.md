# 📰 فصحى — Modern Standard Arabic (MSA)

**Nature:** Not a spoken native dialect — a formal written and broadcast register
**Users:** All Arab countries in formal written contexts: journalism, academia, government, books
**When to use:** Formal documents, pan-Arab content, news, academic writing, government communications

---

## 1. What MSA Is (and Isn't)

### What it IS
- The standardized written Arabic used across all 22 Arab League states
- The language of newspapers, official government documents, academic papers, and formal speeches
- A register that educated Arabs can read and understand regardless of their spoken dialect
- The language of the Quran (Classical Arabic) in modernized form

### What it is NOT
- A spoken vernacular — no one grows up speaking MSA as their mother tongue
- Emotionally warm — it carries formal, detached connotations in conversational contexts
- The right choice for social media posts, captions, personal communications, or emotional copy
- What people mean when they say "write in Arabic" for informal content

**The biggest MSA mistake:** Using MSA for content that should feel personal, emotional, or conversational. It creates distance instead of connection.

---

## 2. When to Use MSA

### Appropriate contexts
- ✅ News articles and journalism
- ✅ Government announcements and press releases
- ✅ Academic writing and research papers
- ✅ Legal documents and contracts
- ✅ Formal corporate reports
- ✅ Religious content (especially Quranic references)
- ✅ Pan-Arab content reaching multiple countries where no single dialect fits
- ✅ Books, novels (though dialect fiction is rising)
- ✅ Subtitles in formal video content
- ✅ Educational content for children (textbooks)

### NOT appropriate contexts
- ❌ Social media captions (unless news/governmental account)
- ❌ Advertising copy (unless deliberately formal)
- ❌ Emotional marketing content
- ❌ Video scripts for personality-driven creators
- ❌ WhatsApp or personal messaging
- ❌ Chatbot conversations
- ❌ Product descriptions targeting mass consumer market

## 3. Key Grammar Reference

### Verb system
MSA preserves the full classical verb system:

**Past tense (فعل ماضٍ):**
كَتَبَ (he wrote), كَتَبَت (she wrote), كَتَبوا (they wrote)

**Present/Imperfect (فعل مضارع):**
يَكتُب (he writes), تَكتُب (she writes), يَكتُبون (they write)

**Future (المضارع + سَـ or سوف):**
سَيَكتُب (he will write), سوف يكتبون (they will write)

**Imperative (فعل أمر):**
اكتُب (write! m.s.), اكتُبي (f.s.), اكتُبوا (m.pl.)

### Dual forms (مثنى)
MSA maintains dual forms that most dialects drop:
- كتابان = two books
- الطالبان = the two students
- يكتبان = the two of them write

### Case endings (الإعراب)
MSA has three cases (nominative مرفوع, accusative منصوب, genitive مجرور):
- الكتابُ (nominative), الكتابَ (accusative), الكتابِ (genitive)
- In formal writing: full case endings required
- In semi-formal (news): sometimes dropped in practice

### Masculine/Feminine agreement
Full agreement system: adjective agrees with noun in gender, number, case.
- الكتابُ الجديدُ (the new book, m.)
- القصةُ الجديدةُ (the new story, f.)

## 4. MSA Vocabulary Register

### Formal transitions (appropriate in MSA)
| MSA phrase | Usage |
|---|---|
| علاوةً على ذلك | furthermore / additionally |
| وفقاً لـ | according to |
| في ضوء ذلك | in light of this |
| مما سبق | from the above |
| يُشار إلى أن | it should be noted that |
| تجدر الإشارة إلى | it is worth noting that |
| على الصعيد نفسه | on the same level |
| في سياق آخر | in another context |

**Important:** These phrases belong in MSA formal writing. They are AI-tells in dialect content. See `humanization-protocol.md` Section 1.

### Common MSA verbs (strong, active, appropriate for formal use)
| MSA verb | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| يُسهم في | contributes to | formal |
| يتضمن | includes / encompasses | formal |
| يُعالج | addresses / treats | formal |
| يُشكّل | forms / constitutes | formal |
| يستدعي | requires / calls for | formal |
| يرتكز على | is based on | formal |
| يُبرز | highlights / underlines | formal |

## 5. MSA for Pan-Arab Content

When content must reach all Arab markets without favoring one dialect:
- Use MSA for the written layer
- Keep sentence structure simple and direct (avoid complex subordinate clauses)
- Avoid lexical choices that sound strongly regional (some MSA words have Masri or Gulf associations)
- If using video: MSA narration over visual content works for all Arab markets
- White Dialect (see white-dialect.md) may serve better than full MSA for semi-formal pan-Arab content

---

## 6. MSA in Different Content Types

### News / journalism
- Full MSA with case endings in quality publications
- AP/Reuters Arabic style: present tense for breaking news leads
- Lead paragraph = key facts first (inverted pyramid)

### Corporate reports
- Annual reports, CSR reports, investor communications
- Heavy MSA, formal vocabulary
- Numbers: always use Eastern Arabic numerals (١٢٣) in body text, Western (123) acceptable in data tables

### Legal documents (see also professional-docs/contracts.md)
- Full classical MSA with case endings
- Precise legal terminology
- Bilingual (Arabic/English) is common in Gulf legal docs

### Academic writing
- Full MSA
- Citation format matters: vary by university/region
- Footnotes and bibliography: standard academic Arabic conventions

---

## 7. MSA Quality Checklist

Before delivering MSA content:
- [ ] All verbs agree with subject in gender and number
- [ ] Dual forms used where applicable (not just plural)
- [ ] Adjective-noun agreement in gender, number, case
- [ ] Definite article (ال) applied consistently
- [ ] Idafa (إضافة) constructions are grammatically correct
- [ ] No dialect vocabulary bleeding in
- [ ] No foreign words without Arabic equivalent (unless it's a technical term)
- [ ] Sentence length varied — no paragraph of all short sentences, no paragraph of all long ones

## 8. MSA Tone Variations

| Context | MSA sub-register | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| News | Journalistic MSA | Short sentences, present tense leads, factual |
| Academic | Academic MSA | Complex sentence structure, citations, precise |
| Legal | Legal MSA | Formulaic, exact, no ambiguity |
| Government | Official MSA | Formal, declarative, authoritative |
| Religious | Quranic-adjacent MSA | Classical markers, reverent register |
| Corporate | Business MSA | Semi-formal, structured, clear |
