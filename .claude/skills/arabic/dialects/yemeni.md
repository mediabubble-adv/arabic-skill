# 🇾🇪 يمني — Yemeni Arabic

**Population:** ~33 million (large diaspora in Saudi Arabia, UAE, UK, USA)
**Sub-dialects:** Sanaani (capital, prestige), Hadrami (eastern/diaspora), Adeni (southern port city, Gulf-influenced), Tihamawi (coastal)
**When to use:** Yemen-targeted content, Yemeni diaspora (especially Gulf), Hadrami diaspora (SE Asia)

---

## 1. Sub-Dialect Overview

### صنعاني (Sanaani — Capital dialect)
The prestige Yemeni variety. Used as default for Yemeni content.
- Strong classical Arabic features preserved (among the most classical of Arabic dialects)
- Traditional, conservative register by default
- Tribal culture deeply embedded in vocabulary

### حضرمي (Hadrami — Eastern Yemen / Hadramawt)
- Famous diaspora dialect: Hadrami communities in SE Asia (Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore), East Africa, Gulf
- Very prestigious in SE Asian Muslim communities (associated with Islamic scholarship)
- More conservative and classical than Sanaani

### عدني (Adeni — South Yemen / Aden)
- British colonial history: some English loanwords
- Gulf influence (close proximity)
- More cosmopolitan than interior
- Socialist/leftist political vocabulary from PDRY era (handle carefully)

## 2. Phonology — Sound Rules

| Feature | Yemeni Arabic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ق (qaf) | q (preserved) | Like KSA/Gulf — NOT glottalized |
| ج (jeem) | g (Sanaani/common) | "جميل" → "gmiil" |
| ف → p | some areas | Tihamawi influence |
| Vowels | More classical preservation | Closer to MSA than Egyptian |
| ن at end | sometimes drops | "مين" → "مي" (informal) |

**Yemeni Arabic preserves more classical features than most Arabic dialects.** This gives it a dignified, somewhat formal feel even in casual speech.

## 4. Grammar Patterns

### Present tense
Yemeni uses a prefix similar to other dialects:
- عم + verb (some areas) or bare present form
- Context carries meaning more than a specific marker

### Negation
- ما + verb: "ما قلت" = I didn't say
- مو: "مو صح" = not right (Adeni)
- مش: used in some areas influenced by Masri TV

### Future
- بـ + verb or رح + verb (Gulf influence in Adeni)
- Context often substitutes formal future marking

## 6. Brand Voice Guide

### Yemeni consumer brands (diaspora focus)
- **Nostalgia is extremely powerful** — diaspora Yemenis have deep homeland longing
- Food, music, traditional crafts = high emotional connection
- Hadrami identity in SE Asia: emphasize Islamic heritage and scholarly tradition
- Conservative social register — family, community, tradition

### Gulf-based Yemeni diaspora
- Large communities in Saudi Arabia and UAE
- Often economically successful businesspeople
- Hadrami merchants: long trading tradition — business credibility language matters
- Conservative in social content, pragmatic in business content

### Humanitarian / NGO context
- Extreme sensitivity required — ongoing conflict since 2015
- Never use conflict as a marketing backdrop
- Focus on resilience, community, rebuilding
- Connect to Yemeni pride in ancient civilization (Sana'a, Ma'rib, the Queen of Sheba legend)

---

## 7. Cultural Context

### Qat culture
Qat is not just a plant — it's the primary social ritual in Yemen (afternoon sessions).
- Never reference qat negatively in content for Yemeni audiences
- The مفراج (qat room) is the center of community discussion, storytelling, business

### Tribal culture
Yemen has a very strong tribal social structure.
- Honor (شرف) and dignity (كرامة) are paramount values
- Content that respects tribal values earns deep loyalty
- Never imply disrespect to family, tribe, or elders

### Ancient civilization pride
Yemen has one of the oldest continuous civilizations in the Arab world:
- Saba'a (Sheba) civilization, Ma'rib Dam
- Coffee was first cultivated in Yemen (الموكا / المخا)
- References to Yemeni heritage = deep pride trigger
