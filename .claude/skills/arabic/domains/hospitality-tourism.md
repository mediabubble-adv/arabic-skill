# ✈️ Hospitality & Tourism — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** High — massive post-COVID recovery; Saudi Tourism Authority spending billions; Arab tourists are world's highest-spend
**Primary markets:** UAE (Dubai tourism powerhouse), KSA (Vision 2030 tourism target: 150M visitors by 2030), Egypt (archaeology + beach), Jordan (Petra, Dead Sea), Morocco (Marrakech), Oman (eco-tourism)
**Audience tiers:** Arab leisure travelers, religious tourists (Hajj/Umrah), family travelers, adventure travelers, luxury travelers, inbound tourism marketing

## 2. Religious Tourism — Hajj & Umrah

This is the single most unique and highest-value Arabic travel content category.

### Who creates Hajj/Umrah content
- **Licensed Hajj operators** (حملات معتمدة) — only licensed operators can sell Hajj packages
- **Hotel chains** near Mecca/Medina
- **Airlines** (Saudia, flynas, flyadeal dominant)
- **Government entities** (Saudi Tourism Authority, Ministry of Hajj)

### Content rules — non-negotiable
- **Never** fabricate or exaggerate hotel proximity to Haram: "5 minutes from Haram" must be literally true
- **License number** must appear in Hajj operator ads: "مرخصة من وزارة الحج والعمرة رقم..."
- **Do not commoditize the sacred:** Promotional language must maintain reverence — no urgency tactics like "book fast!" for Hajj
- **Umrah is year-round** (unlike Hajj); marketing is less restricted
- **Women traveling alone:** Since 2021, Saudi Arabia allows Umrah without Mahram escort — content can address women travelers directly

### Recommended tone for Hajj/Umrah
```
رحلة إلى قلب الإيمان

نرافقك في كل خطوة، بتنظيم يليق بالمناسبة وضيافة تحمل معنى العبادة.

[حملة X] — معتمدة من وزارة الحج والعمرة | رقم الترخيص: [XXX]
```

## 3. Market-Specific Context

### Dubai / UAE
- **Leisure tourism #1 globally:** Dubai positions as "the world" destination — mix of beach, desert, culture, luxury, entertainment
- **Events as content pegs:** Expo, Dubai Shopping Festival (DSF), Dubai Food Festival, Dubai Fitness Challenge are annual content opportunities
- **Arabic-language tourism content** targets Arab source markets (KSA, Egypt, Jordan, Levant) visiting Dubai
- **Tone:** Aspirational, exciting, multi-experience

### KSA — Vision 2030 Tourism
- **NEOM / AlUla / Red Sea / Diriyah:** Four mega-destination brands the Saudi Tourism Authority (STA) is building
- **AlUla:** Ancient history + luxury adventure: Hegra (Nabataean tombs), Maraya Concert Hall, desert glamping
- **Red Sea Project:** Luxury eco-tourism, coral conservation, island resorts
- **Diriyah:** Heritage/history of the Saudi Kingdom founding — cultural tourism
- **Tone:** Pride, discovery, modernity-meets-heritage

### Egypt
- **Ancient history dominance:** Pyramids, Luxor, Aswan — world's most recognized heritage tourism
- **Beach tourism:** Red Sea (Hurghada, Sharm), North Coast — domestic and Arab summer tourism
- **Domestic tourism:** Egypt is a massive domestic tourism market; content must serve both inbound (Arab visitors) and domestic Egyptians
- **Stability framing:** Post-2011 tourism recovery narrative still relevant; security assurance is part of the content

### Jordan
- **Petra:** The #1 tourist attraction; "روز سيتي الوردية" is the brand name
- **Dead Sea:** Wellness/spa positioning
- **Medical tourism:** Overlaps with healthcare (see `healthcare.md`)
- **Wadi Rum:** Eco/adventure tourism
- **Aqaba:** Red Sea diving

### Morocco
- **Marrakech dominance:** Riad experience, souks, Atlas Mountains
- **Cultural richness:** Artisan crafts, spices, Moroccan cuisine — all are content anchors
- **European Arab diaspora source market:** Moroccans in France/Spain/Belgium returning home for tourism — diaspora content angle
- **French code-switching** is natural in Moroccan hospitality content

## 4. Audience Tiers & Register

### Family travelers
- **Register:** L3 dialect (per source market)
- **Tone:** Safe, fun, family-affirming, value-conscious
- **Hooks:** Kids activities, safe environment, family rooms, family packages
- **Platform:** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube

### Luxury travelers (HNW)
- **Register:** L4 or White Dialect
- **Tone:** Understated exclusivity, bespoke, never price-led
- **Hooks:** Private experiences, VIP access, butler service, helicopter transfers
- **Platform:** Instagram (curated), WhatsApp (concierge), email

### Young adults / adventure travelers
- **Register:** L2-L3 per country
- **Tone:** Excitement, discovery, FOMO, social-share-worthy
- **Hooks:** Hidden gems, bucket list, Instagram-worthy, adrenaline
- **Platform:** TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts

### Religious travelers
- **Register:** L3-L4 with reverent tone
- **Tone:** Spiritual, organized, trust-building, comfort-focused
- **Hooks:** Proximity to Haram, accreditation, comfort for elderly, group size

## 7. Cultural Context

- **Hospitality as identity:** Arab culture places enormous weight on being a good host — content from hospitality brands should mirror this value, not just sell rooms
- **Family unit travel:** Arab travel decisions are family decisions; children's happiness is a purchase motivator for parents
- **Halal travel considerations:** Halal food availability, prayer facilities, modest environment — these are purchase criteria for observant travelers; addressing them builds trust
- **Social media travel culture:** Arab travelers (especially Gulf) are among the world's highest social media sharers of travel content — design experiences to be share-worthy
- **Summer vs Eid travel peaks:** Summer = family leisure; Eid = multi-day family trips; Ramadan (late) = religious + leisure hybrid
