# 🏛️ Government & NGO — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** Medium — formal register domain; government is often a client/partner; NGO Arabic content is specialized
**Primary markets:** KSA (Vision 2030 communications), UAE (government innovation), Egypt (public sector largest employer), Jordan (INGO/NGO hub), all Arab League states
**Audience tiers:** Citizens, government-to-government, NGO beneficiaries, donor/grant audiences, public sector B2B buyers

## 2. Government Communication Register

Government content in Arabic is almost exclusively MSA. This is the domain where full formal Arabic is non-negotiable.

### Government-to-citizen communication
- **Register:** Formal MSA (journalistic sub-register — clear, direct, informative)
- **Tone:** Authoritative but accessible; avoid bureaucratic complexity for public-facing content
- **Sentence structure:** Short, factual, action-clear
- **Example pattern:** "يُعلن [Ministry/Authority] عن [announcement]. يُطلب من المواطنين [action] بحلول [date]."

### Modernizing government communication
- Several Arab governments (UAE, KSA) are actively simplifying citizen-facing language
- UAE Smart Government initiative: plain Arabic over bureaucratic Arabic
- KSA "Saudi Vision 2030" communications: modern, aspirational MSA, not old bureaucratic style
- **New government voice:** Shorter sentences, active verbs, citizen-benefit-led ("ستتمكن من..." = you will be able to...)

### Social media for government
- Government social media can use **White Dialect** for accessibility
- Twitter/X: Real-time government communications increasingly use near-White Dialect for speed
- Instagram/Snapchat: Government tourist/lifestyle content uses colloquial dialect with official watermarks
- YouTube: Both MSA (formal announcements) and dialect (documentary/educational content)

## 3. Vision 2030 & National Strategy Language (KSA)

Vision 2030 is the dominant context for all Saudi government content. All KSA institutional content should understand this framework.

### Core Vision 2030 pillars (as used in content)
- **مجتمع حيوي** — A vibrant society (culture, sport, entertainment, Islamic heritage)
- **اقتصاد مزدهر** — A thriving economy (employment, investment, privatization)
- **وطن طموح** — An ambitious nation (governance, accountability, sustainability)

### Key Vision 2030 programs cited in content
- **نيوم** (NEOM) — Futuristic mega-city project
- **رؤية الرياض** (Riyadh Vision) — Capital city development
- **القدية** (Qiddiya) — Entertainment city
- **بروجكت مستقبل** (Project of the Future) — Various
- **هدف / تمهير** — Human capital / employment programs
- **الصندوق الاجتماعي للتنمية** (SDF) — Social development fund

### Tone for Vision 2030 content
- Pride without arrogance — Saudi Arabia is building something unprecedented
- "كن جزءاً من التغيير" (be part of the change) is a common and effective frame
- Future-tense optimism: "سيكون..." (it will be...), "نحو..." (toward...)
- Never criticize Vision 2030 programs — simply do not reference competing narratives

## 4. UAE Government Communication

UAE government communication is the most modern and accessible in the Arab world.

### Key UAE government voice characteristics
- Direct, citizen-service-first
- Multilingual awareness (Arabic + English + many others)
- Digital-first: most government content assumes mobile consumption
- Inclusive tone: addresses residents and citizens, not just nationals
- Innovation-proud: "UAE leads X globally" is a frequent and authentic content frame

### UAE government digital presence context
- UAE.gov.ae, Smart Dubai, ADDA — the key government digital portals
- Dubai Smart City, Abu Dhabi Digital Authority: content should use their terminology
- Smart Dubai's "Happiest City" mission: content tone follows — warm, forward-looking

## 5. NGO Content — Special Considerations

### Operational distinction: advocacy vs service delivery
- **Advocacy NGOs** (human rights, policy, journalism): More careful language required; avoid adversarial framing with governments
- **Service delivery NGOs** (healthcare, education, humanitarian): Focus on beneficiaries; neutral on politics
- **Development INGOs** (UN agencies, international): Use international development vocabulary in Arabic

### Donor-facing content (grant applications, reports)
- Full formal MSA
- Impact data: numbers, percentages, beneficiary counts
- Theory of Change framework in Arabic: "من خلال [activity] سنحقق [output] مما سيؤدي إلى [outcome]"
- SDG alignment: Reference أهداف التنمية المستدامة numbers and goals explicitly

### Beneficiary-facing content
- Simplified Arabic — plain, clear, accessible (L3-L4)
- Dignified tone: never pity-based; beneficiaries are agents, not passive recipients
- Avoid: Photos/descriptions that dehumanize or sensationalize suffering
- Preferred: "نعمل جنباً إلى جنب مع..." (we work alongside...)

### Humanitarian content sensitivity
- **Refugee/displacement content:** Extremely sensitive
  - Use "لاجئون" (refugees) only if UNHCR-defined status applies
  - "نازحون" (IDPs) for internally displaced
  - Never use "فلود من المهاجرين" (flood of migrants) or dehumanizing framing
- **Conflict sensitivity:** In Syria, Yemen, Libya, Sudan — never take sides, focus on humanitarian needs
- **Child protection:** No identifying images of children in vulnerable situations; written consent required

## 6. Political Sensitivity by Market

### Egypt
- Avoid: Criticism of government, military references, Muslim Brotherhood mentions
- Safe: Public service announcements, economic development, cultural heritage

### KSA
- Never: Criticize the royal family, Vision 2030 policies, or Islamic governance
- Safe: Vision 2030 alignment, Saudi pride, national achievements

### UAE
- Never: Criticize UAE leadership or foreign policy positions
- Safe: Innovation, international leadership, tolerance, inclusion messaging

### Jordan
- Never: Palestinian issue politicized framing (Jordan hosts largest Palestinian refugee population)
- Safe: Development, stability, economic content

### Lebanon
- Political situation is extremely polarized (Shia/Sunni/Christian/secular divisions)
- NGO content: strictly humanitarian/development framing; no political actors
- Brand content: avoid sectarian references

### Syria
- Extremely sensitive: Post-2011 war context
- Never take political positions on Assad/opposition
- Safe: Cultural heritage, diaspora identity, humanitarian support

## 8. Cultural Context

- **Arabic hospitality in government:** Arab governments host international events with strong hospitality culture — content for events should reflect this warmth alongside institutional professionalism
- **Consensus decision-making:** Arab governance (especially tribal/family systems in Gulf) values consensus — content framing should acknowledge collective decision-making rather than individualistic "government decides alone"
- **Respect for authority:** Arabic culture traditionally respects government authority — content criticizing government must be extremely carefully framed (usually only appropriate for advocacy NGOs in specific legal contexts)
- **Wasta (connections) and government services:** Common cultural reality in some markets — content should address formal channels without implicitly dismissing informal realities
