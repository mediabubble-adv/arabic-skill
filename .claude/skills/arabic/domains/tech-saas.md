# 💻 Tech & SaaS — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** Highest — largest Arabic digital growth sector (2024–2030)
**Primary markets:** KSA (Vision 2030 tech hub), UAE (Dubai Tech, GITEX), Egypt (Cairo tech scene), Jordan (Silicon Wadi)
**Audience tiers:** Developers, CTOs/CIOs, startup founders, enterprise buyers, end-users (B2C apps)

### Loanwords that ARE standard in Arabic tech content
- API, SaaS, AI, ML, CRM, ERP, UX/UI, MVP, ROI, KPI, dashboard, workflow
- Write them in Latin script inline: "نظام الـ CRM" — don't Arabize unnecessarily
- Exception: government/compliance documents — use full Arabic forms

## 2. Audience Tiers & Register

### Tier 1: Developers / Technical audience
- **Register:** L4-L5 Masri / White Dialect / English code examples
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, no fluff, show don't tell, get to the code
- **Content:** GitHub READMEs, technical blog posts, Stack Overflow-style answers
- **Avoid:** Hollow enthusiasm ("revolutionary solution!"), corporate bloat
- **Sample voice:** "في 3 أسطر كود بس، هتعمل الـ webhook في أقل من دقيقة"

### Tier 2: Founders / Product Managers
- **Register:** L3 dialect or White Dialect
- **Tone:** Strategic, outcome-focused, ROI-aware
- **Content:** Product comparisons, case studies, growth tactics
- **Key hooks:** Time saved, cost reduced, customers won
- **Sample voice:** "اللي خلى فريقنا يوفر 8 ساعات أسبوعياً بالضبط"

### Tier 3: Enterprise Decision Makers (CTO/CIO/CEO)
- **Register:** L4 or Business MSA
- **Tone:** Formal, authoritative, risk-mitigation focused
- **Content:** White papers, executive briefings, security/compliance
- **Key concerns:** Data sovereignty, SLAs, integration, vendor lock-in
- **Platform:** LinkedIn, email, in-person events (GITEX, LEAP, Seamless)

### Tier 4: End Users (B2C apps, consumer tech)
- **Register:** L2-L3 per target country
- **Tone:** Warm, simple, benefit-led
- **Content:** App store descriptions, onboarding, push notifications
- **Platform:** App Store, Google Play, social ads

## 4. Regulatory & Compliance Sensitivities

### KSA-specific
- **SDAIA / PDPL compliance** (Personal Data Protection Law): data handling must be disclosed; "نلتزم بسياسة خصوصية صارمة وفق نظام حماية البيانات الشخصية" is required language for apps handling KSA user data
- **Data residency:** Government and fintech clients require KSA-hosted data ("البيانات مستضافة على أراضٍ سعودية")
- **Vision 2030 alignment:** Mention tech localization, Saudi talent development for enterprise sales ("نساهم في تمكين الكفاءات السعودية")
- Never promise government contract certainty — procurement is unpredictable

### UAE-specific
- **DIFC / ADGM jurisdiction:** For fintech/legal tech, always specify applicable jurisdiction
- **Dubai Data Law compliance:** For apps collecting UAE user data
- **Free Zone framing:** Tech companies in DIFC/DMCC operate under different rules; clarify which entity applies

### Egypt-specific
- **NTRA regulations:** Telecom-adjacent tech must reference National Telecommunications Regulatory Authority compliance if applicable
- **Local payment gateways:** Payment tech must integrate Fawry, PayMob, or Paymob for Egyptian market; mention this explicitly in product content

### Pan-Arab
- **GDPR relevance:** For companies with EU presence, state GDPR compliance — educated Arab tech buyers recognize this
- **ISO 27001:** Enterprise security certification worth mentioning explicitly in Arabic
- **No implied data sharing:** Never phrase data policies in ways that imply third-party sharing without consent

### What to never promise
- "100% uptime" — legally risky; use "99.9% uptime SLA" with specific terms
- "Secure" without qualification — use "encrypted end-to-end" or specific standards
- "AI that thinks like a human" — misleading in regulated markets

## 5. Tone Guide

### B2B SaaS
- Lead with outcome, not feature: "وفّر 40% من وقت فريقك" not "نظام إدارة متكامل"
- Specificity is credibility: numbers, case studies, client logos
- Keep skepticism in mind: Arabic enterprise buyers are relationship-first — content must build trust before selling

### Startup / founder content
- Celebration of shipping is expected and authentic
- Use "رحلتنا" (our journey) framing — Arab audiences connect with founder stories
- Failures can be mentioned if framed as learning: "أكبر درس تعلمناه بعد فشل المنتج الأول"

### Consumer tech
- Benefits over features: "تصوير يجنن" not "ميزة التصوير الاحترافي"
- Social proof is critical: "أكثر من مليون مستخدم في السعودية والإمارات"
- Speed and ease are table stakes — elevate them with specificity

## 7. Cultural Context

### Arab tech culture signals
- **GITEX credibility:** Mention GITEX participation for Gulf clients — it's the prestige signal
- **WhatsApp as enterprise tool:** Arab businesses run on WhatsApp; integrations or WhatsApp-native features are selling points
- **Arabic-first as differentiator:** Most global SaaS tools have poor Arabic UX; native Arabic support is a genuine competitive advantage
- **Wasta (connections) culture:** In KSA and Gulf, who vouches for you matters more than your demo — content should build personal credibility, not just product credibility

### What doesn't land
- Cold, purely transactional content — relationship context is expected
- Pressure tactics ("offer expires in 2 hours!") in enterprise — it reads as unprofessional
- Overclaiming AI capabilities — Arab tech audience is increasingly sophisticated and skeptical
