# 🛒 E-commerce & Retail — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** High — largest Arabic content volume; every brand with an online store needs this
**Primary markets:** Saudi Arabia (#1 Arab e-commerce market), UAE (MENA hub), Egypt (high volume, price-sensitive), Kuwait, Qatar
**Audience tiers:** Online shoppers, retail brands, marketplace sellers, D2C brands, luxury buyers

## 2. Payment Landscape (Critical for CTAs)

### Saudi Arabia
- **STC Pay / Apple Pay / Google Pay / Mada** dominant
- Credit/debit widely used
- COD declining but still available
- BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) growing: Tamara, Tabby are KSA-native
- Mention BNPL options explicitly: "أو قسّمها على 4 دفعات بدون فوائد مع Tamara"

### UAE
- **Apple Pay dominant**; credit cards high penetration
- **Tabby / Tamara** growing BNPL
- COD less common in UAE than KSA/Egypt

### Egypt
- **Cash on delivery (COD) dominant** — 60-70% of transactions
- Fawry, PayMob, Paymob for online payments
- BNPL growing rapidly: ValU, Shahry
- COD framing essential: "الدفع عند الاستلام متاح"

### Kuwait / Qatar / Bahrain
- Credit cards high penetration
- KNET (Kuwait) is the local debit network — mention for local trust
- BNPL via Tamara/Tabby

### Cross-border
- Saudi buyers frequently cross-border shop — address customs/VAT explicitly
- Egyptian buyers are cost-sensitive on shipping costs

This domain is driven by campaign peaks more than any other:

| Campaign | Date | Biggest markets | Arabic content angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ramadan | Moving (lunar) | All Arab | Generosity, gifts, family, night shopping |
| Eid al-Fitr | Post-Ramadan | All Arab | Celebration, new clothes, gifts, joy |
| Eid al-Adha | Moving (lunar) | All Arab | Family, home, hosting |
| White Friday | Late Nov | KSA (Noon) | Saudi equivalent of Black Friday |
| Yellow Friday | Late Nov | UAE (Noon) | UAE equivalent |
| Black Friday | Late Nov | Egypt, Levant | Western framing adopted |
| National Day KSA | September 23 | KSA | Pride, local products, patriotism |
| National Day UAE | December 2 | UAE | UAE identity, local brands |
| Valentine's Day | February 14 | Egypt, Levant, UAE | Romance gifts (subtle in KSA) |
| Mother's Day | March 21 (Arab world) | All Arab | Gifts, appreciation |
| Back to School | August–September | All Arab | Kids, stationery, electronics |

**Ramadan is the #1 e-commerce peak.** Volume surges 2x-4x. Shopping happens late at night (11pm-2am). Messaging should reflect nocturnal culture and generosity themes.

## 4. Audience Tiers & Register

### Mass market / everyday shopping
- **Register:** L2-L3 per country
- **Tone:** Friendly, deal-focused, community feel
- **Hooks:** Price, delivery speed, free shipping, COD availability
- **Platform:** Facebook (Egypt), Instagram, TikTok

### Fashion / lifestyle shoppers
- **Register:** L3, style-conscious
- **Tone:** Aspirational, visual-led, trend-aware
- **Hooks:** New arrivals, limited editions, influencer association
- **Platform:** Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat (KSA)

### Luxury / premium buyers
- **Register:** L4-L5 or White Dialect
- **Tone:** Understated, quality-signaling, exclusivity
- **Never:** Discount framing, urgency tactics, mass-market language
- **Platform:** Instagram (curated), email, WhatsApp (personal shopper)

### B2B / Wholesale / Marketplace sellers
- **Register:** L4 Business Arabic
- **Tone:** Partnership, terms-led, volume focus
- **Platform:** LinkedIn, WhatsApp, email

## 7. Trust Signals — Arabic Market

Trust is the single biggest purchase barrier in Arabic e-commerce. Always include:

### Tier 1 (must-have)
- Return/exchange policy stated clearly: "إرجاع مجاني خلال 14 يوم"
- Payment method logos
- Delivery timeline: "التوصيل خلال 3-5 أيام"
- COD availability (Egypt, some KSA/Jordan)
- Phone number / WhatsApp contact

### Tier 2 (strongly recommended)
- Customer reviews in Arabic
- Number of orders/customers: "أكتر من 50,000 طلب ناجح"
- Secure payment badge
- Brand social media presence mention

### Tier 3 (premium/luxury)
- Authenticity certificates
- Exclusive distribution agreements
- Named brand partnerships
- "أصلي ومضمون 100%"

## 9. Cultural Context

- **COD as trust mechanism:** Especially in Egypt and Jordan, COD is not a payment preference — it's a trust mechanism. Offering it signals you're a legitimate seller.
- **WhatsApp shopping:** Many Arabic e-commerce transactions (especially fashion boutiques) close on WhatsApp. Product content should drive toward WhatsApp CTA.
- **Group buying culture:** Arabs share deals with family/friends extensively — social share mechanics and referral programs outperform single-buyer incentives.
- **Ramadan night shopping spike:** E-commerce traffic peaks between 11pm-3am during Ramadan. Scheduling content for this window is a meaningful tactic.
- **Size/fit anxiety:** Especially for clothing — Arabic buyers need very detailed size guides or live WhatsApp assistance; include this in product descriptions.
