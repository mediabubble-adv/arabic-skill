# ⚖️ Legal Services — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** Medium — specialized, high-value; contracts/legal docs are a professional-docs crossover
**Primary markets:** UAE (DIFC/ADGM, most sophisticated legal market), KSA (rapid legal reform), Egypt (largest Arab legal market by volume), Qatar (QFC legal sector)
**Audience tiers:** Legal consumers (individuals), corporate legal clients, law firms marketing, government legal procurement, legal tech buyers

### UAE-specific legal terms
- **DIFC:** Dubai International Financial Centre — common law jurisdiction within UAE
- **ADGM:** Abu Dhabi Global Market — common law jurisdiction
- **DED:** Dubai Economy and Tourism (previously Department of Economic Development) — business licensing
- **Mainland vs Free Zone:** Every UAE legal discussion must clarify which jurisdiction applies
- **Golden Visa:** Legal residence permit; content discussing it needs current Ministry of Human Resources rules

### KSA-specific legal terms
- **NUPCO, MOMRA, MISA, GAZT:** Government agencies with regulatory roles — reference precisely
- **نظام** (nizām) = regulation/law in Saudi Arabia (not "قانون" which some consider conflicting with Sharia)
- **The Company Law (نظام الشركات)** 2022 reforms are significant recent context
- **Wafa' al-Dain:** Debt settlement concepts in Islamic finance

## 2. Register is Non-Negotiable Here

Legal content has two entirely different registers with zero overlap:

### A. Public-facing legal marketing (law firm / legal service ads)
- **Register:** L3-L4 dialect or White Dialect
- **Tone:** Trustworthy, accessible, problem-solution, empowering
- **Audience:** Individuals with legal problems, SME owners, people in disputes
- **What works:** "تعرف حقوقك" (know your rights), "نحن معك في كل خطوة"
- **What fails:** Full MSA legalese in marketing — it creates distance

### B. Actual legal documents (contracts, agreements, filings)
- **Register:** Full legal MSA — formal, precise, zero ambiguity
- **Tone:** Definitive, not conversational
- **See:** `professional-docs/contracts.md` for the document writing guide
- **Important:** NEVER mix colloquial with formal legal document body

## 3. Market-Specific Legal Context

### UAE
- **Dual legal system:** Federal UAE law (Arabic, civil law) + DIFC/ADGM (English, common law)
- **Bilingual contracts are standard** in commercial UAE — Arabic has legal precedence over English in mainland UAE courts
- **Labor law** is a major content category: Ministry of Human Resources, MOHRE — employees and employers both need this
- **Foreign ownership:** Recent changes (100% foreign ownership in many sectors) create significant content opportunities
- **Golden Visa and Green Visa:** Residence permit legal content is highly searched

### KSA
- **Sharia as constitutional basis:** All law derived from Islamic principles; content cannot contradict Sharia
- **Labor Reform Initiative (مبادرة تحسين العلاقة التعاقدية):** 2021 reforms; employer/employee rights content
- **Company Law reforms 2022:** Major overhaul — foreign investors need to understand the new framework
- **Anti-Corruption Authority (نزاهة):** Compliance content increasingly important
- **Saudization (Nitaqat):** Employment of Saudis requirements — legal compliance content for companies

### Egypt
- **Civil law based on Napoleonic/French law** (unlike Gulf Sharia-influenced)
- **Labor disputes:** Significant volume — Egyptian labor law content is very searched
- **Property law:** Real estate law in Egypt is complex; ownership restrictions for foreigners, new cities regulations
- **Dispute volume:** Egypt has one of the world's highest litigation rates — individual legal help content highly demanded

### Qatar
- **QFC jurisdiction:** Common law for financial/business entities in QFC
- **Labor sponsorship (kafala):** Reforms in 2021; worker rights content increasingly relevant
- **Expat legal needs:** Large expat population with specific legal questions (sponsorship, contracts, family law)

## 4. Islamic Law Intersections

Islamic law (Sharia) directly governs personal status in most Arab countries:

### Areas where Islamic law governs
- Marriage (الزواج) and divorce (الطلاق)
- Inheritance (الميراث) — specific Quranic shares
- Wills (الوصية) — restricted to 1/3 of estate
- Waqf (الوقف) — Islamic charitable endowment
- Child custody (الحضانة)

### Content sensitivity in these areas
- Divorce content: Extremely sensitive in all markets — empathetic, non-judgmental tone
- Inheritance content: Educational framing works best; practical "here's what you're entitled to" angle
- Women's rights: Can address Islamic law and women's rights accurately — do not misrepresent either Islamic principles OR women's legal position
- **Khul' (خلع):** Woman-initiated divorce — increasingly discussed; educate without judgment

## 5. Law Firm Marketing Content

### Tone rules for law firm content
- **Establish expertise first:** CV credentials, notable cases, accreditations
- **Problem-first:** Lead with the client's problem, not the firm's prestige
- **Empathetic:** Legal situations are stressful — content that acknowledges this converts
- **Action-oriented:** "تواصل مع محامينا اليوم" — clear next step
- **No outcome guarantees:** Cannot promise specific case outcomes

### Trust signals for Arabic legal market
- Bar association membership / licenses visible
- Years of experience specific to the jurisdiction
- Client testimonials (with permission)
- Media mentions, cases covered
- Available consultation (مجانية = free initial) conversion

### What to avoid in law firm marketing
- Claiming "best lawyer in X city" without awards substantiation
- Guaranteed outcomes: "سنفوز لك" (we will win for you)
- Undercutting other lawyers publicly
- Oversimplification that distorts legal reality

## 6. Legal Tech Content

Legal tech in Arabic markets is nascent but growing:
- Contract management platforms
- Legal research tools (Arabic case law databases)
- e-Notary services
- Corporate governance platforms

Legal tech content tone: Bridge between tech-accessible and legally credible. The lawyer audience is skeptical of technology replacing judgment; address this directly.

## 8. Cultural Context

- **Wasta (connections) and law:** In some Arab markets, who you know matters in legal processes — content should acknowledge cultural reality without endorsing extralegal behavior
- **Reluctance to litigate:** Many Arabs prefer informal resolution (وساطة) over courts — mediation and arbitration content should address this preference
- **Family disputes:** Divorce, inheritance, custody are deeply sensitive and often involve extended family — legal content must be empathetic, never cold
- **Trust deficit with lawyers:** Many Arabic audiences distrust lawyers (perception of high fees, manipulation) — content that demystifies legal process and provides genuine education builds trust
