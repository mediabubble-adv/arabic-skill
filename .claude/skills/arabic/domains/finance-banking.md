# 💰 Finance & Banking — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** Highest — regulated, high-stakes, highest average transaction value per content piece
**Primary markets:** KSA (SAMA-regulated), UAE (CBUAE, DIFC), Egypt (CBE), Kuwait (CBK), Qatar (QCB)
**Audience tiers:** Retail banking customers, HNWIs, SME owners, institutional investors, fintech founders

| المرابحة | al-murābaha | Murabaha (cost-plus financing) | Most common Islamic product |
| الإجارة | al-ijāra | Ijarah (Islamic leasing) | Real estate / auto |

| السيولة | al-suyūla | Liquidity | Treasury/investment |
| التمويل العقاري | al-tamwīl al-'iqārī | Mortgage / real estate finance | High demand content |
| المعاملات الرقمية | al-mu'āmalāt al-raqmiyya | Digital transactions | Neobank/fintech |
| المحفظة الإلكترونية | al-mahfaza al-iliktrūniyya | E-wallet | Consumer fintech |

### Forbidden/sensitive terminology
| Avoid | Use instead | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| فوائد (interest) | أرباح (returns) | In Islamic banking context — فوائد implies riba |
| قرض (loan) | تمويل (financing) | In Islamic banking context |
| رهن (mortgage/pledge) | تمويل عقاري | Riba connotation in some contexts |
| مضاربة (gambling) | — | Never use for investments — has Islamic prohibition connotation |

**CRITICAL:** Know your client's banking model before writing. Conventional vs Islamic requires completely different vocabulary sets.

## 2. Islamic Finance Rules for Content

Arabic finance content in GCC almost always operates in an Islamic finance context. This is not just vocabulary — it's a framework:

### What Islamic finance content must do
- Frame products as partnership/trade structures, not lending
- Emphasize Sharia compliance certification: "معتمد من هيئة الرقابة الشرعية"
- Use "أرباح متوقعة" (expected returns) not "عائد مضمون" (guaranteed return) — investment guarantees are not permitted
- Religious legitimacy framing increases trust: "وفق أحكام الشريعة الإسلامية"

### What Islamic finance content must NOT do
- Promise fixed returns on investment products
- Use the word فوائد (ribawi interest) for Islamic products
- Imply risk-free investments
- Use gambling metaphors for market behavior

### Conventional banking (Egypt, Lebanon, some Jordan content)
- فوائد is acceptable
- Yield percentages can be stated directly
- Standard international banking language applies
- Still avoid gambling metaphors for markets

## 3. Regulatory Compliance by Market

### Saudi Arabia — SAMA
- All promotional content must include: "هذا المنتج خاضع لتنظيم البنك المركزي السعودي (ساما)"
- Investment products require risk disclosure in Arabic
- No guaranteed return promises on market-linked products
- Capital Markets Authority (هيئة السوق المالية) oversight for investment products
- "نفاذ" (Nafath digital ID) integration is a trust signal for Saudi digital banking

### UAE — CBUAE / DIFC / ADGM
- Licensed entities must display license number in ads
- DIFC/ADGM-regulated entities operate under different rules than mainland UAE — specify
- Consumer finance ads: must state total cost of borrowing in Arabic
- "تحت إشراف مصرف الإمارات العربية المتحدة المركزي" for mainland-regulated products

### Egypt — CBE
- Central Bank of Egypt regulates all banking/fintech
- E-wallet ads must reference CBE license
- Fawry, PayMob, Paymob are CBE-licensed — integration is a trust signal
- Financial inclusion messaging resonates: "خدمات مالية للجميع" (financial services for all)

### Kuwait — CBK
- Conservative regulatory environment; compliance copy is mandatory
- Kuwaiti national identity is strong — localize with Kuwaiti cultural references
- Islamic banking dominates; secular banking content needs careful positioning

### Qatar — QCB
- Qatar Financial Centre (QFC) for regulated entities
- Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) is a local prestige reference
- High HNW concentration — wealth management tone appropriate

## 4. Audience Tiers & Register

### Retail banking customers
- **Register:** L3 dialect (per country)
- **Tone:** Accessible, benefit-led, reassuring
- **Concerns:** Ease of use, fees, safety of money
- **Hooks:** Zero fees, instant transfers, 24/7 service
- **Sample:** "حوّل فلوسك في ثانية، حتى في الإجازات"

### SME / Business banking
- **Register:** L3-L4 or White Dialect
- **Tone:** Partnership-focused, efficiency-driven
- **Concerns:** Cash flow, payroll, lending access, accounting integration
- **Hooks:** Working capital solutions, payment collection, multi-currency

### HNWIs / Wealth management
- **Register:** L4-L5 or Business MSA
- **Tone:** Exclusive, understated, trust-built-over-time
- **Concerns:** Capital preservation, diversification, legacy planning, discretion
- **NEVER:** Price-first messaging, discounts, urgency tactics
- **Sample:** "لأن الثروة تُبنى على الاختيارات الصحيحة، لا على العروض"

### Investors / Capital markets
- **Register:** L4 + technical vocabulary
- **Tone:** Data-led, risk-aware, sophisticated
- **Concerns:** Returns, Shariah compliance, diversification, ESG
- **Platform:** LinkedIn, Bloomberg Arabic, Argaam (Arabic financial media)

## 6. Financial Literacy Framing

Arabic markets have mixed financial literacy levels. Content should meet audience where they are:

### Low literacy: Consumer banking, rural Egypt, first-time investors
- Avoid jargon entirely
- Use analogies from everyday life: "الـ yield زي الإيجار بتاعت فلوسك"
- Infographic-first, numbers-prominent
- Simple CTAs: "افتح حسابك مجاناً"

### Medium literacy: SME owners, young professionals
- Mix technical terms with plain explanation
- Show calculations: "لو استثمرت 10,000 ريال بعائد 5% سنوياً..."
- Case study format works well

### High literacy: Investors, finance professionals
- Full technical vocabulary expected
- Data tables, charts, performance attribution
- Peer-to-peer tone — no condescension

## 8. Cultural Context

- **Islamic finance is identity, not just product:** For GCC audiences, Sharia compliance is a values statement. Content that acknowledges this authentically earns trust faster.
- **Relationship banking still dominant:** Gulf and Levantine HNWIs choose bankers they trust personally, not products with lowest rates. Content must build the person/brand, not just the product.
- **Financial literacy is aspirational:** Being seen as financially savvy is a social status signal in Arab culture. Educational content ("how to invest your first 10k") performs very well.
- **Ramadan finance spike:** Personal financing, savings products, and investment products spike in Ramadan — editorial calendar should plan for this surge.
