# Regional Taboos & Sensitivities

Read this file whenever a taboo scan is required. Apply the rules for the target market.
When uncertain, **flag the taboo in the output** rather than silently avoiding it — this lets the client decide.

---

## 🇸🇦 KSA (Saudi Arabia)

**Hard limits (never write these):**
- Any content that could be read as criticizing the government, royal family, or Vision 2030
- References to interest (ربا / فائدة) in finance content — use "عائد" or "صناديق شرعية" instead
- Romantic content between unmarried individuals, even indirect
- Comparisons between men and women as "superior" or "inferior"
- Any reference to alcohol, gambling, or tobacco as desirable
- Humor about religion, death, or the Prophet

**Use with care:**
- Religious phrases (بإذن الله، ماشاء الله) — appropriate when earned and contextual, not as filler on every line
- Gender roles in marketing copy — focus on capability/achievement, not gender specifically
- Pricing language — showing price increases trust in KSA, but position it as investment/value, not discount
- "Westernized" lifestyle references — urban Saudi youth is modern, but explicit Western-brand aspiration can feel tone-deaf post-2019

**Safe and effective:**
- National pride and Vision 2030 language (thoughtfully, not performatively)
- Family honor and legacy framing
- Achievement, success, and ambition themes
- Halal assurance signals in food, finance, and lifestyle content

## 🇦🇪 UAE

**Hard limits:**
- Criticism of rulers or government (zero tolerance — legal exposure)
- Content that targets or excludes specific nationalities (UAE is ~90% expat — keep it inclusive)
- Any reference to Israel/Palestine as political commentary (UAE has normalization; either stay neutral or avoid)
- Religious debate or comparative religion content

**Use with care:**
- Emirati cultural specifics — if your audience is Emirati vs. pan-Arab expat, the register and levers differ significantly
- Luxury signaling — "quiet luxury" resonates more than "flashy wealth" in 2024+ UAE brand voice
- Gender dynamics — UAE is progressive relative to region; women in leadership contexts is normalized and expected in content

**Safe and effective:**
- Ambition, innovation, world-class aspirations
- Multicultural framing (when appropriate)
- Heritage + modernity balance (works well for Emirati-specific brands)

---

## 🇶🇦 Qatar

**Hard limits:**
- Same political limits as UAE
- Content that could appear critical of FIFA, Qatar's hosting of international events (culturally sensitive)
- Alcohol references

**Use with care:**
- Very similar to UAE but smaller market — pan-Khaliji copy usually works for Qatar unless the brief is Qatar-specific

---

## 🇧🇭 Bahrain / 🇰🇼 Kuwait / 🇴🇲 Oman

- Generally similar to UAE rules with slightly more conservative framing
- Kuwait has strong local identity — pan-Khaliji copy may feel generic; if KW-specific, lean into Kuwaiti humor and pride
- Oman is the most conservative Gulf state; Omani content benefits from subdued, dignified framing

---

## 🇪🇬 Egypt (Masri)

**Hard limits:**
- Direct criticism of the military or government (legal risk)
- Sectarian content (Muslim/Christian tensions — Egypt is ~10% Coptic Christian)
- Extreme political content of any kind

**Use with care:**
- Class sensitivity — Egypt has severe income stratification; avoid content that feels tone-deaf about poverty or excessive wealth
- Religious phrases — very common and natural in Egyptian culture (ربنا يبارك، إن شاء الله), but use contextually
- Body-related content — Masri humor about bodies/weight is common culturally, but in branded content it can alienate audiences

**Safe and effective:**
- Community, family, and "الناس" (people) language
- Humor and relatability — Masri audiences reward brands that make them laugh
- Social proof ("الكل بيعمل كده" / "٥٠٠٠ زبون") — mass adoption signals work well

## 🇱🇧 Lebanon / 🇸🇾 Syria / 🇯🇴 Jordan / 🇵🇸 Palestine (Levantine/Shami)

**Hard limits:**
- Political content related to current conflicts (extremely sensitive given the regional situation as of 2024–2025)
- Syrian conflict references in Syrian-targeted content
- Any content that picks "sides" in regional political disputes

**Use with care:**
- Economic hardship references — Lebanon especially; copy that feels out of touch about money is deeply unpopular
- Lebanese sectarianism — avoid any content that could be read as favoring any religious community
- Palestine references — treat with dignity; any brand that jokes about or commercializes Palestinian identity will face severe backlash

**Safe and effective:**
- Resilience, beauty, and cultural pride themes
- Nostalgia (the region has deep nostalgic culture)
- Community and connection themes

---

## 🇲🇦 Morocco (Darija) / 🇩🇿 Algeria / 🇹🇳 Tunisia (Maghreb)

**Hard limits:**
- Criticism of the monarchy (Morocco — zero tolerance)
- Content perceived as mocking Amazigh/Berber culture in Algeria/Morocco
- Strong pro-Israel content

**Use with care:**
- French language mixing — Darija naturally mixes French words (franglais / arabizi), but a brand's choice to use French vs. Arabic signals cultural position
- Religious framing — Maghreb is devout but Francophone educated classes are more secular; know your audience segment
- Pan-Arab framing — Maghrebi audiences sometimes resist being lumped into "the Arab world" messaging without acknowledging their distinct identity

**Safe and effective:**
- Urban cool and youth identity
- Dual cultural pride (Arab + Amazigh + Mediterranean)
- Humor and wordplay — Maghrebi audiences love clever wordplay

---

## 🌍 Pan-Arab / MSA Content

- Safe default: avoid politics, religion debates, and gender hierarchy entirely
- MSA is used for institutional trust — news, legal, educational, official brand comms
- Never use MSA for emotional or community-driven content — it creates distance
- Religious phrases in MSA should be minimal and formal when used (e.g., press releases)

---

## Taboo Scan Checklist (Run Before Every Delivery)

- [ ] Does the content criticize any government, ruler, or political institution?
- [ ] Does it reference alcohol, gambling, tobacco, or interest-based finance without appropriate framing?
- [ ] Does it contain romantic content that could be inappropriate in the target market?
- [ ] Does it involve body-related humor that could alienate the audience?
- [ ] Does it use religious phrases — are they earned and contextual, not performative?
- [ ] Does it pick sides in any regional political or sectarian dispute?
- [ ] Does it present gender dynamics in a way that could backfire in the target culture?
- [ ] Does it use "quiet luxury" vs. "loud luxury" appropriately for the market?

If any box raises a flag: note it in the output and let the client decide.
