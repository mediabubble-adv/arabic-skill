# 🧬 Humanization Protocol — Anti-AI Code

Apply after every content draft, before final delivery.
The goal: the reader never suspects a machine touched this.

---

## The Core Diagnosis

AI-generated Arabic content fails in 4 detectable ways:
1. **Over-formality:** MSA grammar bleeding into dialect content
2. **Banned phrases:** A specific lexicon of AI-isms that no native speaker uses in speech or informal writing
3. **Rhythm regularity:** Sentences that are suspiciously even in length and structure
4. **Emotion labeling:** Telling the reader what to feel instead of creating the feeling

This protocol kills all four.

---

## 🚫 Section 1: Banned Lexicon

These phrases appear in AI Arabic at an unnatural frequency. A native speaker might use one occasionally — they never string them together. If you see any of these in the output, rewrite the sentence from scratch.

### Structural Fillers (Delete entirely)
- في ظل التطورات المتسارعة — (delete + rephrase the actual point)
- وتجدر الإشارة أن / تجدر الإشارة هنا إلى — (delete + say the thing directly)
- من المهم الإشارة إلى أن — (delete + say the thing directly)
- لا يخفى على أحد أن — (delete + say the thing directly)
- هذا يقودنا إلى — (delete; just go to the next point)
- وفي هذا السياق — (delete + restructure)
- على صعيد آخر — (overused transition; find a better one)
- من جهة أخرى — (only use if you're literally presenting two sides; not a filler)
- وختاماً / في الختام — (delete entirely; let the ending land without announcing itself)
- خلاصة القول — (delete; start the actual summary)

### Hollow Praise (Delete and replace with specifics)
- جودة عالية ومميزة — (every brand says this; it means nothing)
- حل شامل ومتكامل — (delete; name the specific capability)
- منظومة متكاملة — (delete; describe what it actually does)
- خبرة واسعة — (delete; cite the specific number of years, clients, or projects)
- بكل احترافية — (show don't tell; what specifically makes it professional?)
- على أعلى مستوى — (every vendor says this; skip it)
- نقدم لكم — (delete the opener; start with the benefit)

### Corporate Hedges (Delete or rewrite to commit)
- كن متأكداً من أن / rest assured — (delete; just make the claim)
- لا يمكن إنكار أن — (delete; say the thing)
- من المحتمل أن — (commit or don't; half-claims are worse than no claim)
- قد يكون من المفيد — (commit: this IS useful, or cut it)
- بشكل أو بآخر — (delete; be specific)
- بطريقة ما — (delete; be specific)

### Over-Used Verbs (Replace with stronger, more specific verbs)
- يتسم بـ / يتميز بـ → (show the characteristic directly instead)
- يتمحور حول → (say what it's about; don't meta-describe)
- يُعدّ / يُشكّل / يُسهم في → (rewrite actively: X does Y, not X is considered to contribute to Y)
- يُتيح لنا → (rewrite: X lets you do Y)
- يستدعي → (rewrite: requires, needs, demands — pick the right word)

### AI Cadence Phrases (Rewrite)
- أولاً... ثانياً... ثالثاً — (fine for structured documents; NOT for social, scripts, or emotional copy)
- لا شك أن / دون أدنى شك — (the doubt is in the deleted phrase, not in the reader)
- ليس هذا فحسب، بل — (sounds translated; rewrite the structure)
- على وجه التحديد — (overused as filler; only keep if it's genuinely adding precision)
- خطوة نحو — (vague; say what the actual action is)

## 🎵 Section 2: Rhythm Rules

AI text has suspiciously even rhythm. Real writers vary it. These rules break that pattern.

### The Three-Length Rule
After any 2 sentences of similar length, the next one must be shorter or longer — deliberately.
Vary: **short.** A medium sentence that adds context. **And sometimes one long sentence that builds the full image so the reader can feel the weight of the idea before moving on.**

### The Non-Parallel Structure Rule
AI loves parallel structures: "إنت بتشتغل أكتر، بتتعب أكتر، بتدفع أكتر."
Break the pattern in one of the three: "إنت بتشتغل أكتر، بتتعب — وبعدين إيه؟"

### The False Start Rule
Real writers sometimes start in the wrong direction and correct themselves. Add controlled false starts in informal content:
- "فكرت أقول كده... لأ، الأصح إن..."
- "الحل — أو بالأحرى، البداية — هي..."
- "مش هنجيب مثال. هنجيب اتنين."

### The Sentence Count Variation
Don't let every paragraph have 3 sentences. Mix: one paragraph = 1 sentence. Next = 4. Next = 2. 

### The Transition Replacement Rule
Never use "أولاً / ثانياً / ثالثاً" in emotional, informal, or creative content. Replace with:
- Masri: "طب والتانية؟" / "وكمان حاجة تانية" / "بس الأهم"
- Khaliji: "وثاني شيء" / "والأهم من كذا" / "بس الشيء الأهم"
- Levantine: "والشغلة التانية" / "وهون الأهم"
- MSA formal: Only context where numbered lists are genuinely appropriate

## 🎭 Section 3: Show-Don't-Tell Emotion

### The Rule
Never label the emotion. Create the situation that produces the emotion.

| ❌ Telling (AI habit) | ✅ Showing (Human writing) |
|---|---|
| "شعرت بسعادة غامرة" | "ضحكت لحالي في السيارة" |
| "هذا المنتج سيجعلك سعيداً" | "لما توصلك الشنطة، هتبقى غير" |
| "الموقف كان صعباً ومؤلماً" | "ما قدرتش أنام تاني يوم" |
| "شعرنا بالفخر الشديد" | "والله ما عرفنا نوصف الإحساس ده" |
| "هذا شعور رائع" | "بتحس إنك صح" |
| "ستشعر بالراحة التامة" | "هتنام من غير ما تفكر" |
| "خبرة استثنائية" | "ما تلاقيش زي ده في مصر كلها" |

### For Brand Content
Don't tell the reader what they'll feel. Create the scenario that produces the feeling:
- ❌ "منتجنا سيمنحك الثقة بالنفس"
- ✅ "لما تلبسيه أول مرة، هتتمنيلك طريقة تدخلي بيه كل اجتماع"

### For Educational Content
Don't tell the reader the content is valuable. Demonstrate the value immediately:
- ❌ "هذا المقال سيغير نظرتك إلى [topic] بشكل جذري"
- ✅ Open with the most surprising or counterintuitive fact. Let the reader feel the shift.

## ✨ Section 4: Imperfection Quotient

Controlled imperfection is a sign of craft. These are deliberate, not errors.

### Approved Imperfections (Use 1–2 per output, not more)

**1. The Trailing Thought (...):**
Signals thinking — not conclusion.
- "والموضوع أكبر من كده..."
- "وبعدين... ما قلتلكش حاجة"

**2. The Self-Interruption:**
The writer catches themselves and redirects. Feels alive.
- "كنت هقول [X] — بس ده مش الكلام الصح. الكلام الصح هو [Y]."
- "مش هنكمل في النقطة دي. في حاجة أهم."

**3. The One-Word Paragraph:**
One word. Alone. On a line.
```
وبعدين؟

لا حاجة.
```
Use when you want a beat of silence. Maximum one per piece.

**4. The Fourth-Wall Break:**
Direct address that acknowledges the reader is reading:
- "وانت عارف اللي بتكلم فيه" (Masri)
- "وانت فاهم قصدي" (Khaliji)
- "ومش لازم أكمل" (Levantine)

**5. The Comma Splice (Breath Pause):**
In Arabic informal content, a comma can replace a full stop for a breath effect:
- "حلو، بس مش كافي"
- "فكرة صح، التنفيذ غلط"

**6. The Question as an Answer:**
When the answer is too obvious to state, ask it back instead:
- Someone asks: "هل الاستثمار في المحتوى مهم؟"
- Response: "هو بجد في سؤال هنا؟"

## 🌍 Section 5: Dialect-Specific Humanization

### مصري / Masri
**Add these markers for authenticity:**
- والله العظيم / بجد / فعلاً — before the key claim (signals genuine conviction)
- طب as a thought transition ("طب والسبب؟ / طب ولو؟")
- ما + double negative for emphasis: "ما عارفتش ما أقولش"
- Trailing questions: "مش كده؟ / وأنا باقول إيه؟"
- Sentence-internal exclamation: "بقالها سنين — سنين بقولك! — وما حدش فاهم"

**Remove these MSA bleed indicators:**
- يُعدّ / يُشكّل / يُسهم — rewrite as simple active verbs
- Formal subject-verb-object order in ALL sentences — vary it
- Long relative clauses ("الذي يُعدّ من أبرز...") — cut and say the thing directly

### خليجي / Khaliji
**Add these markers for authenticity:**
- بصراحة — signals directness; use before an honest take
- والله — as an opener for genuine statements, not religious emphasis
- يا الله — as an exclamation of mild surprise/appreciation
- ش at end of negation: "ماعرفش / ماقدرش" instead of "ما أعرف / ما أقدر"

**Remove:**
- Masri فعلاً as intensifier (sounds jarring in Khaliji)
- Egyptian slang in Khaliji scripts — even if the brand uses both markets
- Over-casual abbreviations (LOL, etc.) — Khaliji informal is warm, not internet-casual

### شامي / Levantine
**Add:**
- هيك = that's how / like this (as a casual connector)
- يعني as a thinking-aloud filler mid-sentence (signals naturalness)
- بعدين as a soft transition ("بعدين... شو صار؟")
- No before adding nuance: "لأ، شو قصدي..."
- مناحي / شو / هون — distinctly Levantine vocabulary that signals authenticity

**Remove:**
- Masri فعلاً, يابني, أيوه — sounds foreign in Levantine
- Gulf aspirational tone — Levantine tone is more intimate and lyrical

### مغربي / Darija
**Add:**
- راه — signals current state (راه قلت = I already said)
- والو = nothing (for emphasis of emptiness or dismissal)
- Code-switching French words where natural: c'est bon, hadi = هادي
- كيف داير / لاباس as opener for warm casual content

**Remove:**
- Full MSA paragraphs in Darija content — it signals inauthenticity to the audience
- Gulf or Masri vocabulary that Moroccan audiences would mark as foreign

### عراقي / Iraqi
**Add:**
- گ as a marker of authentic speech (گلبي = قلبي, گاعد = قاعد)
- أبوية / أمي as casual referential terms (like "man" in English)
- Proverb-heavy content for emotional or philosophical points — Iraqi culture prizes poetic reference
- Dark humor as a humanizer — Iraqis have a distinct, acknowledged dark comedy tradition

**Remove:**
- Egyptian TV Arabic that doesn't exist in Iraqi speech
- Gulf luxury vocabulary in contexts that don't fit the Iraqi audience economic register

## 🔁 Section 6: Anti-Translationese Layer

The deepest AI tell: Arabic that is English wearing an Arabic costume. Detect and rewrite.

### Detect
- **English-first sentence logic:** subject→verb→object marketing order, "Discover how X can transform your Y" structures, lead-with-the-verb CTAs ("اكتشف"، "حوّل"، "أطلق العنان").
- **Adjective stacking:** "حل مبتكر وفعّال وسهل الاستخدام" — English piles adjectives; Arabic prefers one strong image.
- **Abstract corporate framing:** "نحو مستقبل أفضل"، "نعيد تعريف…" — translated from English deck-speak.
- **Direct-lift rhetorical structures:** "ليس فقط… بل أيضاً" (not only… but also), "تخيّل لو…" (imagine if…), "ماذا لو أخبرتك أن…" (what if I told you…).

### The Back-Translation Test (decisive)
Translate the line literally to English:
- Reads as **natural English marketing** → **translationese ✗ → rewrite**
- Reads **awkward in English** but meaning lands → **native Arabic ✓**

| ❌ Translationese | ✅ Native |
|---|---|
| "اكتشف كيف يمكن لمنتجنا أن يحوّل عملك" | "منتجنا هيغير شغلك — وهنوريك إزاي" |
| "نحن نؤمن بأن الجودة تأتي أولاً" | "الجودة عندنا مش كلام — جرب وانت تحكم" |
| "ليس فقط سريع، بل أيضاً موثوق" | "سريع، وكمان ما بيخذلكش" |

Rewrite from the **Arabic idea**, not the Arabic words of an English sentence.

## 📡 Section 7: Channel Humanization Layer

Humanization rules shift by channel. Apply the channel column on top of Sections 1–6.

| Channel | Rhythm | Length | Markers | Avoid |
|---------|--------|--------|---------|-------|
| **Captions / social** | punchy, fragments OK | 1–3 short lines | hook in first 5 words, 1 emoji max if brand allows | filler openers (نقدم لكم), numbered lists |
| **Video / scripts** | spoken cadence, breaths | read-aloud timed | direct address, false starts, beats | written-only connectors (وتجدر الإشارة) |
| **Landing pages** | scannable, varied | short paras + 1 long | concrete proof, scene-based emotion | hollow praise, adjective stacks |
| **WhatsApp** | conversational, 1 idea/msg | very short | name use, 1 question, soft CTA | corporate hedges, formal salutations |
| **LinkedIn** | professional-warm | medium | specific numbers, 1 lived detail | slang, internet-casual, hype |
| **Tutorials** | calm, sequential | step-sized | imperative voice, "هتعمل…", checkpoints | motivational fluff, abstract intros |
| **Dev docs** | precise, neutral | tight | code stays English, explanation in target register | marketing tone, emotion words |

**Rule:** code, commands, and identifiers stay English even in dialect prose; only the *explanation* takes the dialect/register.

## ✅ Section 8: Self-Test

Run all before delivering:

**Test 1 — The Read Aloud Test:**
Read the content aloud in the target dialect. If you stumble on any word or phrase, that's MSA bleed or AI vocabulary. Rewrite that sentence.

**Test 2 — The 3-Second Test:**
Read just the first 3 sentences. Would a native speaker from that region guess this was written by an AI? If the answer is yes, the hook needs rewriting.

**Test 3 — The Banned List Scan:**
Search the output for any phrase from Section 1. If found: delete the sentence, rewrite from a different angle.

**Test 4 — The Rhythm Check:**
Count sentence lengths in words for any 5 consecutive sentences. If the variation range is less than 5 words (all sentences are 10–15 words), the rhythm is too even. Break it.

**Test 5 — The Emotion Audit:**
Highlight every emotion word in the text (سعادة, فرح, خوف, راحة, etc.). Each one must be earned by a scene, image, or situation. If it's floating without context, delete it and show the scene instead.

**Test 6 — The Back-Translation Test:**
Translate the strongest line literally to English. If it reads as natural English marketing copy, it's translationese — rewrite from the Arabic idea (Section 6).

**Test 7 — The Channel Fit Test:**
Check the output against its channel row in Section 7. Wrong rhythm/length/markers for the channel = rewrite to fit (e.g., a LinkedIn-length paragraph dropped into a WhatsApp message).
