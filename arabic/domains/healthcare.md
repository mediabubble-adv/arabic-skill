# 🏥 Healthcare & Medical — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** High — highly regulated, emotionally sensitive, rapidly growing (telemedicine, health tech)
**Primary markets:** KSA (Vision 2030 health sector), UAE (Dubai Health Authority), Egypt (Ministry of Health), Qatar (Hamad Medical), Jordan (medical tourism hub)
**Audience tiers:** Patients, caregivers, healthcare professionals (HCPs), hospital administrators, health tech buyers

## 2. Regulatory Compliance by Market

### KSA — Ministry of Health / SFDA
- **Saudi Food and Drug Authority (هيئة الغذاء والدواء):** All drug/supplement/medical device claims must be SFDA-approved
- **SFDA mandatory disclaimer:** "يُرجى مراجعة الطبيب قبل استخدام هذا المنتج" — required for any medical product
- **No diagnosis claims:** Cannot state a product "treats," "cures," or "prevents" any disease without clinical evidence and regulatory approval
- **Telehealth: SEHA Virtual Hospital** is the national reference for approved telemedicine in KSA — mention compatibility/integration for credibility
- **Women's health:** Increasingly permissible to discuss openly post-Vision 2030; still frame with cultural sensitivity

### UAE — Dubai Health Authority / DOH
- **DHA license number** required in all healthcare provider ads
- **Abu Dhabi DOH** has separate licensing requirements from DHA
- **HAAD** (now DOH Abu Dhabi): healthcare advertising rules require licensed provider attribution
- **Medical claims:** Must be substantiated; no superlatives like "أفضل مستشفى" without awards/rankings evidence

### Egypt — Ministry of Health
- **No treatment guarantees** for any condition
- **Supplement content:** Must distinguish clearly between supplement and pharmaceutical
- **Medical tourism source market:** Egypt is a source of patients for Jordan/Turkey — content targeting Egyptians for medical tourism is a significant niche

### Jordan — Medical Tourism Hub
- Jordan is a **regional medical tourism destination** (especially cardiac, oncology, orthopedics)
- Content targeting GCC patients coming to Jordan: emphasize accreditation (JCI certification), Arabic-speaking staff, international standards
- **JCI accreditation** is the gold standard signal: "معتمد دولياً وفق معايير JCI"

### Pan-Arab
- **Never diagnose:** "قد يدل على..." (may indicate) not "هذا يعني أن لديك..."
- **Mental health sensitivity:** Discuss with care — use "الصحة النفسية" not "الجنون" or stigmatizing terms
- **Alternative medicine:** Cupping (حجامة), herbal medicine (الطب العشبي) are culturally valued — can mention alongside, never instead of, medical consultation

## 3. Mental Health — Special Guidance

Mental health is the fastest-growing and most sensitive healthcare content area in the Arab world.

### What has shifted (post-2020)
- Younger Arabs (18-35) are actively discussing anxiety, depression, burnout
- Saudi and UAE markets have the highest mental health content engagement
- Mental health apps (Shezlong in Egypt, Kalaam in KSA/UAE) have normalized the conversation

### Language that works
- "ضغط نفسي" (psychological pressure) — normalized, non-stigmatizing
- "صحتك النفسية بنفس أهمية صحتك الجسدية" — educational framing
- "الاستشارة النفسية" (psychological consultation) — more accessible than "العلاج النفسي"
- "إرهاق عاطفي" (emotional exhaustion) — Gen Z resonant

### Language to avoid
- "مجنون" / "مجنونة" — stigmatizing, never use
- "مريض نفسي" — clinical label, use only in clinical contexts
- "ضعف" (weakness) framing — never imply mental health struggles indicate weakness
- Content that dismisses mental health with religious platitudes alone (can include faith perspective, but alongside professional help)

### What never changes
- Always end mental health content with a referral signal: "إذا كنت تمر بتجربة صعبة، التحدث مع متخصص قد يساعد"

## 4. Audience Tiers & Register

### Patients (B2C)
- **Register:** L3 dialect (per country)
- **Tone:** Warm, reassuring, empowering — not alarmist
- **Concerns:** Pain, fear, cost, waiting times, trust in provider
- **Content:** Symptom education, when to see a doctor, procedure explainers, testimonials

### Caregivers (mothers, family members)
- **Register:** L3 dialect with emotional warmth
- **Tone:** Supportive, informative, practical
- **Especially relevant for:** Pediatric, elderly care, chronic disease management
- **Emotional hook:** "أهلك يستاهلون أحسن رعاية" (Your family deserves the best care)

### Healthcare Professionals (HCPs)
- **Register:** L4-MSA with clinical vocabulary
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, evidence-based, no marketing fluff
- **Content:** CME credits, clinical guidelines, conference invites, journal summaries
- **Platform:** WhatsApp groups (dominant), medical apps, LinkedIn

### Hospital Administrators / B2B Health Tech
- **Register:** L4-L5 Business Arabic
- **Tone:** Efficiency, compliance, cost-reduction focused
- **Content:** EMR/EHR integration, hospital management systems, accreditation support

## 6. Tone Guide

### Patient-facing
- Lead with empathy, not information: "نعرف إن الألم صعب. وأنت ما لازم تمر بيه لوحدك."
- Never minimize symptoms or dismiss concern
- Always recommend professional consultation — never replace it
- Testimonials work extremely well: real patients, real outcomes

### Condition-specific sensitivity levels
| Condition | Sensitivity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cancer | High | Never "treatment guarantees" |
| Mental health | High | Anti-stigma framing always |
| Fertility / IVF | High | Sensitive in religious context |
| Diabetes / chronic | Medium | Very prevalent; content well-received |
| Cardiovascular | Medium | High burden, receptive audience |
| Dental | Low | Relatively comfortable topic |
| Aesthetic / cosmetic | Low-Medium | Popular in GCC; judgment-free framing |
| Sexual health | Very High | Extremely carefully framed; marital context only in KSA |

## 8. Cultural Context

- **Doctor as authority:** Arabic culture traditionally gives high deference to medical professionals — content that features real doctors (or doctor-like figures) converts well
- **Family decision-making:** Major health decisions are often family decisions, not individual ones — address the family unit, not just the patient
- **Religious framing:** "الجسم أمانة" (the body is a trust) — Islamic framing of healthcare responsibility resonates deeply
- **Private vs public care:** Private hospitals are aspirational status symbols in Egypt and Levant; in GCC, quality private care is the norm for most
- **Cosmetic/aesthetic medicine:** Massive and growing in KSA, UAE; no judgment culture in content — treat it as any other healthcare category
