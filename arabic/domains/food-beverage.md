# 🍽️ Food & Beverage — Arabic Content Domain

**Priority:** Medium-High — universal need; Ramadan/Eid drives massive peaks; food delivery booming
**Primary markets:** All Arab markets (universal); KSA and UAE for premium restaurant/delivery; Egypt for volume
**Audience tiers:** Home cooks, restaurant goers, food delivery users, F&B brands, chefs/influencers, Ramadan content audience

## 2. Halal Content — Non-Negotiable

**Every meat/food product in Arabic content requires halal status to be explicitly clear.**

### What to say
- "حلال 100%" — the simplest statement; not sufficient alone for skeptical buyers
- "ذبح على السنة المحمدية" — slaughtered per Islamic tradition (resonant in Egypt/Levant)
- "شهادة حلال معتمدة من [authority]" — certified by recognized authority
- For UAE: ESMA Halal certification is the gold standard
- For KSA: SASO / SFDA approved halal certification

### What to never imply
- Any ambiguity about pork: absolutely zero mention, never jokingly, never "it's like halal bacon"
- Alcohol in cooking: never. Some cuisines use wine in sauces — omit or use halal alternatives without comment
- Cross-contamination risk: premium halal brands explicitly address "no pork cross-contamination" for Gulf buyers

## 3. Ramadan F&B Content — Major Annual Peak

Ramadan is the single biggest food content moment in the Arab calendar. Volume multiplies 3-5x.

### Ramadan food content structure

**Ramadan timing:**
- **Suhoor (سحور):** Pre-dawn meal — content focus: sustaining energy through the fasting day; slow-release carbs, protein, hydration
- **Iftar (إفطار):** Breaking fast at sunset — content focus: traditional breaking foods (dates + water first), then soups, then mains, then sweets
- **Late-night dining:** Many Arabs eat again at 10pm-midnight during Ramadan — content can address this third "meal"

**Traditional Ramadan foods (content pegs by country):**
| Country | Iconic Ramadan food | Content angle |
|---|---|---|
| Egypt | Fattah, Keshk, Om Ali, Qatayef | Nostalgia, mother's cooking, tradition |
| KSA | Kabsa, Thareed, Harees, Sobia | Heritage, family gathering |
| UAE/Gulf | Harees, Machboos, Luqaimat | Tradition + modern twist |
| Lebanon/Levant | Katayef, Sahlab, Atayef | Sweet specialties |
| Morocco | Harira soup, Chebakia, Msemen | North African identity |

**Tone for Ramadan food content:**
- Generosity and family are the emotional core — food is love, food is sharing
- "على مائدة رمضانك" (at your Ramadan table) — connects product to sacred family space
- Never pressure or hustle tone during Ramadan — warmth and patience
- Recipes that honor tradition are more trusted than Western-fusion innovation during Ramadan

## 4. Regional Cuisine Identity

Each Arabic region has strong cuisine identity. Content that acknowledges and honors this earns trust.

### Egyptian cuisine
- **Identity foods:** Koshari, Ful medames, Molokhia, Fatta, Om Ali
- **Coffee culture:** Tea is #1; Arabic coffee less dominant; Nescafé widely used
- **Pride trigger:** "أكل البيت المصري مفيش زيه" (Egyptian home cooking has no equal) — this statement will get engagement
- **Street food culture:** Koshary, liver sandwiches, koshari — street food content is massively shared

### Saudi / Gulf cuisine
- **Identity foods:** Kabsa (national dish), Mandi, Harees, Saleeg, Jareesh
- **Coffee culture:** Arabic coffee (قهوة عربية) with cardamom — deeply cultural; Saudi coffee ceremony is identity
- **Date culture:** Dates (تمر) are central — gifting, breaking fast, daily eating. Date content is evergreen
- **Camel milk:** Growing premium product with health positioning in Gulf

### Levantine cuisine
- **Identity foods:** Hummus, Fattoush, Kibbeh, Mansaf (Jordan), Maqlouba
- **The hummus wars:** Ownership of hummus is hotly contested between Lebanon, Palestine, Syria — avoid taking sides
- **Bread culture:** Pita/khubz is central to every meal
- **Olive oil:** Levantine olive oil pride is strong — "زيت زيتون بلدي" (local olive oil) is a premium signal

### Moroccan cuisine
- **Identity foods:** Tagine, Couscous, Bastilla, Harira
- **Mint tea ceremony:** The Moroccan tea ceremony (atay) is a hospitality ritual
- **Spice identity:** Ras el Hanout, Argan oil — Moroccan specialty products for diaspora content

## 5. Food Delivery — Specific Content Rules

Food delivery (توصيل الطعام) is a massive and growing Arabic content category.

### Key selling points by market priority
1. **Speed:** "30 دقيقة أو أقل" — the golden promise
2. **Real-time tracking:** "تتابع طلبك على الخريطة" — transparency = trust
3. **COD availability:** Especially Egypt — "ادفع عند الاستلام"
4. **Minimum order clarity:** Arabic buyers hate surprise minimums — state upfront
5. **Halal restaurant signage:** Delivery apps should show halal status per restaurant

### Delivery platforms context
- **UAE:** Talabat, Deliveroo, Careem Now
- **KSA:** HungerStation, Jahez, Talabat
- **Egypt:** Talabat, Otlob, Elmenus
- Content targeting these platforms: use their promotional language norms and CTA formats

## 7. Tone Guide

### Restaurant marketing
- Don't over-describe: "لحم ذهبي محمر على نار هادئة" works better than a list of adjectives
- Sensory language: sight, smell, sound, texture — food writing must engage senses
- Occasion framing: "لعشاء لا يُنسى" (for an unforgettable dinner) — occasion drives restaurant choice

### Recipe content
- Step-by-step structure: Arabic recipe audiences want clarity and repetition
- "أمي بتعمله كده" (my mom makes it this way) — home-cooking authenticity drives engagement
- Ingredient availability: reference regionally-available ingredients, not Western substitutes

### FMCG / packaged food
- Benefit-led for health-conscious: "بدون مواد حافظة / بدون سكر مضاف"
- Value-led for price-sensitive (Egypt): "وفّري مع العيلة"
- Premium/occasion for Gulf: "لمائدة تليق بضيوفك"

## 9. Cultural Context

- **Food as love language:** In Arab culture, feeding someone well is a primary expression of love and care — "كل" (eat!) is said with deep affection. Content that taps into this emotional register performs very well.
- **Generosity as value:** الكرم (generosity) in hosting and feeding guests is a foundational Arab value — content that frames food as an act of generosity resonates culturally.
- **Mother's cooking idealization:** "أكل ماما" (mama's cooking) is the gold standard for taste in Arabic culture — homemade, traditional cooking is aspirational even in restaurant content.
- **Dates as sacred:** Dates (تمر) breaking the fast in Ramadan, and offered to guests in Gulf culture — date content carries enormous cultural resonance.
- **Never waste framing:** Islamic tradition strongly discourages food waste — sustainable, portion-appropriate, or "use all of it" content has cultural legitimacy.
