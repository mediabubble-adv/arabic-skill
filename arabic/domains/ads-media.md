**Priority:** High — revenue engine for agencies, performance marketers, and every D2C brand
**Primary markets:** Egypt (high volume, price-sensitive, COD-driven), Saudi Arabia (high budgets, Snap/TikTok strong), UAE (premium, English-mixed)
**Audience tiers:** Performance marketers, ad agencies, SME owners running their own ads, brand managers
**Pair with:** `references/ads-service-matrix.md` (per-platform specs), `references/seasonal-calendar.md`, the target `dialects/` file

## 1. Egyptian Paid-Media Psychology

The Egyptian buyer is **skeptical, price-aware, and socially-driven**. Ads must overcome three reflexes:

1. **"ده إعلان" reflex** — instant ad-blindness. Native, un-polished, face-led creative beats studio gloss.
2. **"هينصب عليّا" reflex** — fear of being scammed. COD, reviews, real phone/WhatsApp, and a real face defuse it.
3. **"غالي" reflex** — price objection before value is understood. Anchor value or reframe price (per-day, per-use) early.

**Core principle:** Egyptians buy from people, not brands. Put a human, a voice, or a WhatsApp number in the funnel.

## 3. The Egyptian Ad Funnel

| Stage | Goal | Channels | Message psychology |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Stop the scroll | TikTok, Reels, YouTube, Meta | Tension/curiosity, native feel, a face |
| **Consideration** | Build trust | Meta feed, Display, retargeting | Proof: reviews, numbers, demos, "ليه احنا" |
| **Conversion** | Close | Google Search, click-to-WhatsApp | Remove friction: COD, free shipping, easy reply |
| **Retention** | Repeat | WhatsApp broadcast, retargeting | Loyalty, exclusivity, "عرض خاص لعملائنا" |

## 4. Hook Psychology (Egypt)

Hooks that consistently work:

- **The price reframe:** "بتدفع تمن قهوة في اليوم وانت مغمض" (per-day anchoring)
- **The shared pain:** "كلنا عملنا الغلطة دي في [X]"
- **The number:** "3 حاجات بتخليك تخسر فلوس في إعلاناتك"
- **The contrarian:** "بطّل تعمل [common advice] — ده بيضرّك"
- **The local insider:** "لو عندك بيزنس في مصر، الكلام ده ليك"

Hooks that fail in Egypt:
- Brand-name openers ("شركة X تقدم…")
- Generic superlatives ("الأفضل في الشرق الأوسط")
- Western-translated enthusiasm ("اكتشف الفرق المذهل")

## 6. Retargeting & Funnel Discipline

- **Never** show the same creative to cold and warm audiences. Cold = hook/awareness; warm = proof/objection-handling; hot = offer/urgency.
- **Sequence the objection:** awareness raises desire → consideration answers "أصلي؟ مضمون؟" → conversion answers "إزاي أدفع؟".
- **COD is a retargeting weapon** in Egypt — surface it strongest at the conversion stage.
- **Frequency caps matter:** Egyptian audiences sour fast on repetition; rotate creative weekly.

## 8. Compliance & Taboo Notes (Ads-Specific)

- **No fake scarcity** that breaks trust ("آخر قطعة" repeated daily destroys credibility).
- **Health/finance claims** must avoid guarantees — see `domains/healthcare.md` and `domains/finance-banking.md`.
- **Ramadan tone:** generosity and warmth, never exploitation of religious sentiment — run `references/taboos.md` + `references/seasonal-calendar.md`.
- **Gendered targeting** must respect local norms; avoid copy that shames or pressures.

---

## 9. Cultural Context

- **WhatsApp closes the sale** in Egypt — design every funnel to hand off to a human conversation.
- **Comments are social proof** — an ad with lively Arabic comments outperforms a "clean" one; seed and reply.
- **Influencer + paid** stacking is standard: boost creator content (Spark Ads) rather than studio ads.
- **Price transparency** earns trust in a market burned by hidden fees — "السعر شامل كل حاجة" is a hook.
