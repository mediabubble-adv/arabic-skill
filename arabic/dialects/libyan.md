# 🇱🇾 ليبي — Libyan Arabic

**Population:** ~7 million | **Diaspora:** Egypt, Tunisia, Malta, UK, Italy
**Sub-dialects:** Tripolitan (west, Tripoli), Benghazi/Cyrenaican (east), Fezzani (south)
**When to use:** Libya-targeted content, Libyan diaspora, North African campaigns that include Libya

---

## 1. Sub-Dialect Overview

### طرابلسي (Tripolitan — Western Libya, Tripoli)
The commercial capital's dialect. More Maghrebi-influenced.
- Closer to Tunisian Arabic geographically and phonetically
- Italian loanwords from colonial period (1911–1943)
- More cosmopolitan historically

### برقاوي / بنغازي (Cyrenaican — Eastern Libya, Benghazi)
- Closer to Egyptian Arabic than Tripolitan
- More conservative, tribal influence
- Post-2011: strong regional identity pride

### فزّاني (Fezzani — Southern Libya)
- Strong Berber/Tuareg influence
- More isolated, distinct vocabulary
- Less relevant for commercial content

**Post-2011 political context:** Libya is divided between east and west. Content must be politically neutral. Avoid references to specific armed groups, governments, or political factions.

## 5. Brand Voice Guide

### Libyan consumer brands
- Warmth and directness — Libyan communication is warm but no-nonsense
- Family and hospitality values (similar to across North Africa/Arab world)
- POLITICAL NEUTRALITY IS MANDATORY — never signal allegiance to either Tripoli or Benghazi governments/factions
- Italian loanwords in Tripolitan content signal local authenticity (use naturally)
- Oil wealth context: Libya has significant natural resources — premium goods are accessible to middle/upper class

### Libyan diaspora (Egypt-based)
- Large Libyan community in Egypt, especially Alexandria and Cairo
- Nostalgia for pre-2011 stability is a common emotional touchstone
- Food, family, specific Libyan dishes = identity anchors
