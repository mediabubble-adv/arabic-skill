# 🇸🇩 سوداني — Sudanese Arabic

**Population:** ~45 million | **Diaspora:** Egypt, Gulf, Europe, North America
**Dialect character:** Warm, melodic, strong hospitality culture, East African-influenced
**When to use:** Sudan-targeted content, Sudanese diaspora content, East African Arab market

---

## 1. Dialect Overview

Sudanese Arabic occupies a unique position: influenced by Egyptian Arabic (via trade and media), classical Arabic (Quranic tradition is strong), and sub-Saharan African languages (Nubian, Nilo-Saharan). The result is a warm, musical dialect with distinct vocabulary.

**Khartoum dialect** is the prestige variety. Omdurman has slight variations. Northern Sudanese near Egypt sounds closest to Egyptian Arabic.

---

## 2. Phonology — Sound Rules

| Feature | Sudanese Arabic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ق (qaf) | g (like Iraqi/some Gulf) | "قلب" → "galb" |
| ج (jeem) | g or j | "جميل" → "gimiil" |
| ث/ذ | t or s / d or z | Simplified like other dialects |
| Vowels | More open, musical quality | Distinct melodic quality |
| Long vowels | Often preserved | More classical retention than Masri |

## 5. Brand Voice Guide

### Sudanese consumer brands
- Hospitality and generosity are CORE values — brands that embody this earn loyalty
- Family-first messaging: extended family (أسرة) as the unit, not individuals
- Music and poetry: Sudanese love music deeply — audio elements in content matter
- Conservative social values: avoid any content that pushes social boundaries
- Warmth, warmth, warmth — Sudanese communication is among the warmest in the Arab world

### Diaspora content (Sudan)
- Egypt has the largest Sudanese diaspora community
- Gulf-based Sudanese: professionals, service workers — aspirational content resonates
- Sudan is undergoing political transition since 2019 — diaspora follows news closely
- Emotional homeland connection: food (كسرة, أسيدة, ملاح) and specific places trigger nostalgia

### Cultural specificity
- Sudan is geographically and culturally between the Arab world and East Africa — this is a feature, not a tension
- Nubian culture and identity: celebrated, not erased
- Sudanese coffee culture (جبنة / coffee with spices) = pride trigger for diaspora

## 7. Cross-Dialect Contamination

| Avoid | Belongs to | Replace with |
|---|---|---|
| أيوه (dominant) | Masri | أي / تمام / آه |
| إيه (what) | Masri | شنو / ماذا |
| هيك | Levantine | كده / هكذا |
| بزاف | Moroccan | وايد / كثير |
| أكو/ماكو | Iraqi | فيه / ما فيه |
| هواه | Yemeni | وايد / كثير |
| يعني (overloaded) | Levantine | بس / كده |
