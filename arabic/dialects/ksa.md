# 🇸🇦 سعودي — Saudi Arabic (KSA)

**Population:** ~35 million native Saudis | **Economic weight:** Largest Arab economy
**Sub-dialects:** Najdi/Riyadhi (Central, dominant), Hejazi (Western/Jeddah/Mecca), Qassimi (North-Central)
**When to use:** KSA-targeted content, Gulf premium campaigns, Vision 2030 context

---

## 1. Sub-Dialect Map

### نجدي / ريّاضي (Najdi — Riyadh & Central)
The prestige Saudi dialect. Most commercial content uses this as the baseline.
- Hard ق preserved (not glottalized)
- ك → ch before front vowels in some words (گ/ك variation in informal speech)
- Conservative, tribal undertone, direct

### حجازي (Hejazi — Jeddah, Mecca, Medina)
More cosmopolitan, historically the merchant coast.
- Softer than Najdi
- More foreign loanwords (Turkish, South Asian influence via hajj trade)
- Less conservative in cultural register
- Young Jeddawi audience: more receptive to bold/Western-influenced creative

### قصيمي (Qassimi — Al-Qassim region)
Strong tribal identity, deeply conservative. Agricultural and trading heritage.
- Very traditional vocabulary
- Highest formality expectations in commercial content
- Religious language woven naturally into everyday speech

**For most commercial content:** Default to Najdi/Riyadhi. If Jeddah-specific, you can lean Hejazi.

## 2. Phonology — Sound Rules

| Classical/MSA | KSA equivalent | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ق (qaf) | q (preserved) | Unlike Egypt — Saudis keep the hard q |
| ج (jeem) | y (Najdi) / g (Hejazi) | "جميل" → "yimeel" (Najdi) / "gimeel" (Hejazi/informal) |
| ك before i/e | ch (informal) | "كيف" → "cheef" in informal Najdi |
| ث (tha) | th (preserved more than Egypt) | — |
| غ | ɣ (uvular fricative) | Similar to MSA |
| Long ā | often raised | كتاب → "kitab" or "ketab" |

**Key distinction from Egyptian:** ق is NEVER glottalized in formal KSA content. Using glottal stop marks the content as Egyptian.

## 4. Grammar Patterns

### Present tense (no بـ prefix — differs from Masri)
KSA uses the present tense stem without the Egyptian بـ prefix for habitual actions:
- يكتب = he writes (habitual, not necessarily in progress)
- Context + adverb marks timing

### Future tense
- بـ + present: "بيكتب" → NOT used in KSA
- KSA future: رح + verb (Najdi informal) or بيكون + verb
- "راح أكتب" = I will write (Najdi)
- "بكرة أكتب" = tomorrow I write (simple future by context)

### Negation
| Pattern | KSA | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ما + verb | ما كتب | didn't write |
| مو | مو زين | not (for nouns/adj) |
| لا | لا تكتب | don't write (command) |
- Note: NO double negation sandwich (unlike Masri ما...ش)
- "ما كتب" is complete — no ش at the end

### Questions
- Yes/no: "كتبت؟" (rising intonation)
- Specific: وين (where), متى (when), ليش (why), شلون (how), مين (who), إيش / ماذا (what)

## 5. Register Scale

| Level | Description | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| L1 — Badawi/Tribal | Bedouin/rural traditional | Traditional brands, heritage, food |
| L2 — Sha'bi Najdi | Informal street/family | Mass market, affordable goods |
| L3 — Riyadhi Standard | Educated urban professional | Most commercial content |
| L4 — Hejazi casual | Jeddah youth/cosmopolitan | Lifestyle, fashion, entertainment |
| L5 — Professional | Corporate/B2B | B2B, government contracts, formal |

**Note:** Mixing Hejazi and Najdi in the same piece sounds inauthentic. Pick one based on audience.

## 7. Brand Voice Guide

### Consumer brands (general KSA)
- Warmth through hospitality language: يعطيك العافية, ما قصّر
- Direct benefit statements over flowery language
- Pride in Saudi identity: reference local landmarks, culture, seasons (طيف, الربيع السعودي)
- Vision 2030 alignment when authentic (not forced)
- Avoid any content that could be read as questioning traditional values

### Luxury brands
- Najdi prestige register (L4)
- بصراحة as directness signal works well for premium
- Frame in رقي (refinement) and أصالة (authenticity)
- Male audiences: status/excellence framing. Female audiences: quality/self-care framing.

### Jeddah / Hejazi brands
- Slightly more playful and cosmopolitan
- Can reference the sea, Red Sea coast, diverse heritage
- More receptive to international references
- Still conservative — no relationship content

### Startup / Tech brands
- L4 Najdi with digital vocabulary
- Emphasize ريادة الأعمال, مستقبل رقمي
- Reference Vision 2030 honestly — Saudis can tell when it's forced
- Young Saudi founders are sophisticated and skeptical of corporate speak

## 8. Cultural & Religious Context

### Religion in content
- إن شاء الله is genuine in KSA — not ironic. Use when talking about future plans.
- بسم الله is appropriate at the start of major announcements
- Ramadan content shifts: spiritual overtones, giving, family
- Avoid: music in video ads (use it tastefully if needed), mixed-gender social scenarios, alcohol references even obliquely

### Gender-specific content
- B2B and tech often male-coded. Use masculine defaults unless product is female-specific.
- Female audiences are rapidly expanding (driving, entertainment, workforce). Content for Saudi women: empowerment within cultural framework, not Western feminism framing.
- Women address: يا أختي / حبيبتي — both work

### Vision 2030 context
Major cultural shift under way. References to:
- Entertainment: concerts, sports, tourism are all positive signals
- Female workforce participation: encouraged
- Saudi entertainment industry (films, music, gaming): growing fast
- International investment: genuine pride topic

## 10. Cross-Dialect Contamination — Avoid These

| Phrase | Dialect | Replace with in KSA |
|---|---|---|
| ألب (ء for ق) | Masri | Use hard ق: قلب |
| إيه | Masri | إيش / ماذا / شنو |
| أيوه | Masri | أيه (rare) / إي / نعم |
| يابني / يا عم | Masri male address | أخوي / صاحبي |
| بجد / فعلاً | Masri | بصراحة / والله |
| مش | Masri negation | مو / ما |
| طب / طيب (as transition) | Masri | زين / حسناً |
| هيك | Levantine | كذا / بهالطريقة |
| بالله عليك | Levantine | بالله / يا الله |
