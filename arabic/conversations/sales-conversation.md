## 1. Arabic Sales Culture — Foundations

Before any script, understand the cultural operating system:

### Relationship first, transaction second
Arab sales culture is relationship-driven. Jumping to pitch without rapport is the fastest way to lose the sale. The buyer needs to trust you as a person before they trust your product.

**The sequence:** Rapport → Curiosity → Need Discovery → Offer → Close
**Western mistake:** Curiosity → Offer → Close (skips relationship; fails in Arab markets)

### Wasta and vouching
In Gulf markets especially, a referral from someone the prospect trusts is worth more than any pitch. Always ask: "مين عرّفك بينا؟" (who told you about us?) and build on that bridge.

### Negotiation is expected
In most Arab markets, the first price is the opening bid, not the final offer. Content should leave room for this socially important ritual — especially in Egypt and Levant.

### Silence is not rejection
Arab conversation has comfortable silence. Don't fill silence with panic-talking. A prospect who goes quiet is thinking, not saying no.

## 2. WhatsApp Sales Sequence — 5-Message Framework

WhatsApp is the primary sales channel across the Arab world. Structure:

### Message 1: The Opener (Day 0)
```
مرحبا [Name] 🙂

أنا [Your name] من [Company name].

وصلني إنك مهتم بـ [product/service] — صح؟
```
- Keep it short — 2-3 lines max
- End with a question to confirm interest; gets a response
- No pitch yet — just open the door

### Message 2: Discovery (same day, after response)
```
تمام!

عشان أقدر أساعدك صح، عايز أفهم أكتر:

— إيه اللي بتدور عليه بالظبط؟
— وإيه المشكلة اللي حاسسها دلوقتي؟

خليني أفهم ثم أقولك بصراحة لو إحنا مناسبين ليك.
```
- Frame as helping, not selling
- "أقولك بصراحة لو مناسبين" builds trust: signals you won't push if there's no fit

### Message 3: The Offer (next day, after discovery)
```
بناءً على اللي قلته:

[Brief customized solution — 3 lines max]

النتيجة المتوقعة: [specific outcome]

الأسعار تبدأ من [X] — وعندنا خيارات تناسب حسب احتياجاتك.

تحب نتكلم 10 دقائق على الفون أو على واتساب وأشرحلك أكتر؟
```
- Connect offer directly to what they said in discovery
- State a price range to qualify (not lose time on wrong-fit prospects)
- End with a low-commitment CTA (10 minutes, not "sign up now")

### Message 4: Follow-up (3 days after no response)
```
مرحبا [Name]

فاكرني من قبل؟ 😊

بس عايز أطمن عليك — وصلت رسالتي ولا في حاجة محتاج توضيح فيها؟
```
- Soft, non-pressuring
- "طمن عليك" (checking on you) not "following up" — relationship framing
- Emoji is acceptable in WhatsApp; softens the follow-up tone

### Message 5: Final close (5 days later, last contact)
```
[Name] آخر رسالة وبعدين مش هضايقك 🙂

لو مش الوقت المناسب دلوقتي — في أي وقت برجع ليك.

بس لو خايف تجرب أو عندك سؤال، قولي — وأنا هنا.

كل ده مبنتاش عليه حاجة، بس يسعدني لو قدرت أساعدك.
```
- No pressure — signals respect for their time
- "مش هضايقك" (I won't bother you) — relieves social discomfort of ignoring messages
- Leaves door open genuinely

## 3. Phone Sales Script Framework

### Opening (30 seconds)
```
— مرحبا، أنا [Name] من [Company].
— بتكلمك في وقت مناسب؟

[Pause — let them answer. If yes, continue. If busy, ask when to call back — don't push.]

— معظم الوقت بيكلمني [Name/referral/how they found you]، وقالي إنك مهتم بـ [X].
```

**Rules:**
- Always ask permission to speak — skipping this is rude in Arab culture
- Mention the connection/referral immediately if you have one
- Speak slowly; don't race through a pitch

### Discovery (2-3 minutes)
```
قبل أي حاجة — خليني أفهم وضعك أكتر.

دلوقتي، إيه أكبر تحدي بتواجهه في [relevant area]؟
وإيه اللي جربته قبل كده؟
ولو في حل مثالي، بيبقى إيه؟
```

**Discovery rules:**
- 3 questions max before they feel interrogated
- Take notes audibly — "ماشي، فاهم" signals you're engaged
- Reflect back: "يعني إنت بتقول إن المشكلة الأساسية هي [X]"

### Offer pivot
```
بناءً على اللي قلته — عندنا بالظبط اللي بتحتاجه.

[State the offer in one sentence]

هتساعدك في [outcome1] و[outcome2].

وعندي سؤال واحد بس: إيه اللي هيوقفك من إنك تجرب؟
```
- One sentence offer, two outcomes
- The question at the end surfaces objections early — better than closing into resistance

### Handling the most common Arabic objections

**"غالي علي" / "مش متوفر ميزانية" (Too expensive)**
```
فاهم. وعشان كده عايزك تفهم إيه اللي بتجيبه بالسعر ده.

لو اشتغلنا مع بعض وعملنا [outcome]، هتكون عملت [ROI].

السؤال مش كم بتدفع — هو: بتدفع مقابل إيه؟
```

**"محتاج أفكر" (Need to think about it)**
```
طبعاً. ولو بتفكر معناها عندك سؤال أو قلق — قولي بصراحة.

إيه اللي بيوقفك بالظبط؟
```
— Never fight this objection; open it instead

**"مش محتاجه دلوقتي" (Not now)**
```
ماشي — سؤال واحد بس: إيه اللي هيتغير عشان يبقى الوقت مناسب؟
```
— Gets you real timeline or reveals it's actually a different objection

**"رجاعك لأهلي/شريكي" (Need to consult)**
```
ده منطقي جداً. وعشان تسهّل عليك المحادثة معاهم، نكون مع بعض في ال call؟

ولا بعتلك ملخص بالنقط الرئيسية تعرضها عليهم؟
```
— Don't fight the gatekeeper; co-opt them

## 4. Dialect Guide for Sales Conversations

| Context | Dialect | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Egyptian B2C WhatsApp | Masri L2-L3 | Warmth, peer-feel |
| Saudi B2C (phone/WhatsApp) | KSA L2-L3 | Respect + local familiarity |
| Gulf B2B | Khaliji L3 or White Dialect | Professional but regional |
| Pan-Arab B2B email | White Dialect or Business MSA | Neutral, formal |
| Levantine clients | Levantine L3 | Regional match (if calling Lebanon/Jordan) |
| Luxury / HNW | L4 any dialect or White Dialect | Understated, not pushy |

**Rule:** Match the prospect's dialect in their opening message. If they write Masri, respond in Masri. If they write formal Arabic, respond formally. Mirror their register within your script.

## 5. Ramadan Sales Adjustments

Sales during Ramadan requires specific adaptation:

- **Tone shift:** Less aggressive; Ramadan is a spiritual month — hard-sell feels disrespectful
- **Timing:** Most Arabs are more receptive after Iftar (evening) and before Suhoor (late night)
- **CTA softening:** "فكّر معانا" not "اشتري دلوقتي"
- **Generosity framing:** "عرض خاص لرمضان" — seasonal gift not pressure tactic
- **Avoid:** Calling during Dhuhr or Asr prayer times

## 6. What Never Works in Arabic Sales

- Hard close with artificial urgency in first message ("العرض بيخلص الليلة!") — seen as manipulation
- Impersonal opener ("dear customer") — relationship requires a name
- Overselling before trust: "أحسن منتج في السوق!" before knowing if they need it
- Pressure after "no" — damages relationship permanently; in Arab culture, forced compliance = future boycott
- Formal MSA in WhatsApp B2C chat — it's cold and robotic; kills the warm sales channel
