# 📚 Book Writing — Arabic Long-Form Project Mode

**Load when:** The task is a book, guide, serial long-form outline, chapter plan, or `/arabic book`.
**Pair with:** `references/project-mode.md`, `references/advisory-mode.md`, `references/output-templates.md`, `references/humanization-protocol.md`, and `references/taboos.md`.

> **Rule:** Never draft a full book in one shot. Build the premise, structure, and continuity first.

---

## 1. Book Workflow

Run the workflow in order:

1. **Premise** — what the book is about in one clear sentence
2. **Promise** — what the reader gets by finishing it
3. **Audience** — who it is for and what they already know
4. **Outline** — the full chapter map
5. **Chapter blueprint** — purpose, tone, and key beats for each chapter
6. **Sample voice** — a short sample that proves the register
7. **Drafting** — write chapter by chapter
8. **Continuity QA** — names, terms, claims, and tone stay consistent
9. **Final cohesion review** — check that the whole book still feels like one work

---

## 2. Intake Questions

Ask only what changes the book:

- What is the core promise of the book?
- Who is the reader?
- What stage is the project in right now?
- What tone or register should carry through the book?

If the user already has an outline, skip directly to chapter blueprint and continuity checks.

---

## 3. Structure Rules

### Premise

Write one sentence that states:

- the subject
- the transformation
- the reason the book matters now

### Outline

The outline must be MECE:

- no duplicate chapters
- no missing bridge chapters
- one job per chapter

### Chapter Blueprint

For each chapter include:

- chapter title
- purpose
- key argument or narrative beat
- tone
- expected length

---

## 4. Continuity Ledger

Carry a ledger for the whole book:

- proper names
- key terms and preferred translations
- recurring metaphors
- claims and numbers
- chapter-to-chapter promises
- emotional register

If a term is introduced once, keep it stable unless there is a strong reason to change it.

---

## 5. Sample Voice

Before drafting the full book, produce a short sample section that shows:

- the opening voice
- the level of formality
- how examples are handled
- how transitions sound in Arabic

This prevents the outline from drifting into a voice the final manuscript cannot sustain.

---

## 6. Continuity QA

Check the manuscript for:

- contradictions across chapters
- repeated points that should have been merged
- terms that change meaning mid-book
- tone drift between chapters
- unsupported claims or invented detail

If the book uses project evidence, apply `references/project-context-scanner.md` before writing any claims about a real product or repo.

---

## 7. Delivery Format

For a book project, deliver in this order:

1. premise and promise
2. audience and tone
3. full outline
4. chapter blueprint
5. sample voice
6. drafted chapter or chapters
7. continuity notes
8. next-step recommendation

If the user only wants planning, stop before drafting and keep the deliverable scoped to structure.
